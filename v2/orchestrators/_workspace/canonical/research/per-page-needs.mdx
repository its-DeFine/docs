# Per-Page Content Needs

What each page must deliver to its reader, derived from the canonical audience design (S1-S12 ideal sections, personas, jobs, journey stages). Organised by nav section group, in docs.json order.

**Key**: Each page maps to an audience need. The task for each page is: what content must be present for the reader to arrive in one state and leave in the next?

---

## Start Here

### portal.mdx - Portal
- **Audience need**: "I just landed on this tab - orient me fast"
- **Journey stage**: `discover`
- **Entry state**: Arrived from Home, About, or search; may not know what an orchestrator is
- **Exit state**: Understands the tab's scope; knows where to go next based on their situation
- [ ] Hero framing that answers "what is this tab for?" in one sentence
- [ ] Visual or link map to the three entry paths: evaluate first, jump to setup, explore AI workloads
- [ ] Cross-tab inbound context for readers arriving from Delegators or About tabs

### navigator.mdx - Navigator
- **Audience need**: "Which path is mine?" (maps to S1 disambiguation)
- **Journey stage**: `discover`
- **Jobs served**: J2 (identify the right path)
- **Entry state**: Has GPU hardware; does not yet know if they should go solo video, AI-focused, dual mode, or pool node
- **Exit state**: Has identified which path applies and navigated to the correct starting point
- [ ] Decision tree or interactive flow: hardware + capital + goals -> recommended path
- [ ] Make visible that AI-only path does NOT require LPT stake (entry blocker resolution)
- [ ] Make visible that pool node path does NOT require LPT stake
- [ ] Route capital-constrained readers to pool or AI-first path before they hit the LPT wall
- [ ] Link each path outcome to the correct next page (not just a section group - the specific page)

## Concepts

### role.mdx - Role
- **Audience need**: "What does an orchestrator actually do on this network?"
- **Journey stage**: `evaluate`
- **Jobs served**: J1 (assess qualification and earning potential)
- **Entry state**: Knows they have GPU hardware; vague on what Livepeer orchestrators do
- **Exit state**: Can describe the orchestrator role (receive jobs from gateways, process compute, earn fees + rewards)
- [ ] One-paragraph role definition using canonical terminology (orchestrator, gateway, service fee, inflation reward)
- [ ] Distinguish orchestrator from gateway, delegator, and developer roles
- [ ] Make clear this is a supply-side role - you provide compute, gateways send you work

### capabilities.mdx - Capabilities
- **Audience need**: "What can my node actually do - video, AI, or both?"
- **Journey stage**: `evaluate`
- **Jobs served**: J1, J2
- **Entry state**: Understands the role; does not know the workload types or their different requirements
- **Exit state**: Knows the two workload types (video transcoding, AI inference) and that dual mode exists
- [ ] Video transcoding: what it is, requires active set membership (stake-based routing)
- [ ] AI inference: what it is, capability-based routing, does NOT require active set
- [ ] Dual mode: both simultaneously, different routing for each
- [ ] VRAM as the key hardware constraint for AI pipeline selection

### architecture.mdx - Architecture
- **Audience need**: "How does work actually reach me and how does the system fit together?"
- **Journey stage**: `evaluate`
- **Jobs served**: J1 (maps to S3 - how work reaches you)
- **Entry state**: Knows the role and workload types; does not understand routing, payments, or system topology
- **Exit state**: Can describe stake-based routing (video) vs capability-based routing (AI), probabilistic micropayments, and the O-T split architecture
- [ ] Two routing paths: stake weight for video, capability advertisement for AI
- [ ] Gateway -> orchestrator -> worker flow
- [ ] O-T split concept (orchestrator process vs worker process)
- [ ] Probabilistic micropayments: tickets, TicketBroker, win probability (high-level)
- [ ] Service URI: why reachability matters for receiving work

### incentive-model.mdx - Incentive Model
- **Audience need**: "How do I actually get paid - and how much could I earn?"
- **Journey stage**: `evaluate`
- **Jobs served**: J1 (viability assessment - maps to S2 + S3)
- **Entry state**: Understands the architecture; does not know the economics
- **Exit state**: Can describe the two income streams, what reward cut/fee cut affect, and has a rough viability framework
- [ ] Two income streams: ETH service fees (per job) + LPT inflation rewards (per round)
- [ ] Reward cut and fee cut: what they are, who they affect, trade-off with delegation attractiveness
- [ ] Reward call: active operational requirement, not passive - miss it and you forfeit that round
- [ ] Active set economics: stake weight determines rank, rank determines video job share
- [ ] AI economics: no stake requirement, pricing and capability determine job volume
- [ ] Honest framing: what hardware + capital combination is needed for meaningful income
