## INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Progressive Disclosure
Each journey flows through three levels:
- **Portal/Overview** — What is this? Which path applies to me?
- **Concept/Landing** — Explain this area. Here's what you can do.
- **Reference/Action** — Links, lists, detailed guidance, external surfaces.

### 2. Scannable Structure
- Headings and subheadings reveal the page structure without reading prose
- Tables, bulleted lists, and one-sentence purpose statements per section
- Minimal prose; links prioritized over explanation

### 3. Links as First-Class Content
A correct link to the Forum governance category is more valuable than a 500-word description of what the Forum is. Cross-reference with direction.

### 4. Handoff Clarity
Every page includes a "Go Deeper" or "For full mechanics see..." section directing readers to owned content in other tabs. No duplication.

### 5. Staleness Signals
Pages with volatile content (active workstreams, RFP lists, event schedules) include `{/* REVIEW */}` comments. Staleness risk is mitigated by linking to live sources (Forum, Discord, calendar, GitHub) rather than documenting current state.
