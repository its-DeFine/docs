## THE PURPOSE OF THIS TAB

The Community tab is the network's **participation infrastructure** — the place that converts visitor interest or curiosity into active engagement in the Livepeer ecosystem. While every other tab has a functional purpose (build, operate, stake, govern), the Community tab's purpose is connective: to answer the question every cross-role participant eventually asks—*"How do I get more involved?"*—and route them into the right structure to do it.

**Specifically, this tab:**

- **Orients** newcomers to how this community is structured, governed, and engaged — the Foundation, workstreams, SPEs, channels, and norms
- **Activates** participants who want to contribute code, documentation, translation, governance, support, or ideas — with funded pathways to do so
- **Connects** community members to the surfaces where Livepeer actually happens — Discord, Forum, events, GitHub, X — without replacing those surfaces
- **Retains** existing participants by keeping them informed: governance, active initiatives, upcoming events, and ecosystem developments

This is not a links dump. It is not a social media aggregator. It is the narrative and structural layer that makes the network feel like a community rather than a protocol.

---

## WHAT "DONE" LOOKS LIKE

A visitor has successfully used this tab when they:

- **Know where to go** — they can find the right platform for their question or contribution in under 2 minutes
- **Take one concrete action** — they join a Discord channel, make a contribution, vote on a proposal, or attend a call
- **Find funded opportunities** — they can identify funded pathways (grants, RFPs, workstreams) without asking another person

**Success metric:** Visitor leaves with a confirmed next step and knows who or where to ask if they get stuck.

---

## AUDIENCE SCOPE

The Community tab serves **all six human types in the Livepeer ecosystem** with different entry points and needs:

1. GPU Operators — seeking governance context and community intel
2. Gateway Operators — seeking coordination and peer networks
3. Developers — seeking contribution pathways and funded opportunities
4. Delegators — seeking governance guidance and community connection
5. Community Participants — primary audience; seeking to contribute or stay informed
6. Evaluators — seeking evidence of community health and maturity

**Note:** This tab is the only multi-persona tab by design. It is the connective tissue between all roles.

## KEY CONSTRAINTS AND HANDOFFS

**Content NOT owned by this tab:**

- Governance mechanics, voting how-to, delegation — **LP Token tab**
- Protocol governance model, LPT token specifications — **About tab**
- SPE mechanics as protocol feature — **About tab**
- Code contribution, grants, RFPs, bug bounties — **Developers tab**
- Role-specific tools (orchestrator dashboards, gateway monitors) — **Orchestrators / Gateways tabs**

**Staleness rule:** Information changing faster than a quarterly review cycle routes to a live source (Forum, Discord, calendar link) rather than static documentation.
