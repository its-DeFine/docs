## FRAMEWORK

The Community tab serves multiple personas with distinct needs. Rather than create a false primary persona, we rank personas by **how squarely the tab's navigation and content serves their needs**. All five are legitimate; ranking drives information architecture emphasis.

---

## PERSONAS RANKED 1–5

### Rank #1: The Network Participant Seeking Connection

**Who they are:** An existing participant — delegator, orchestrator, developer, or interested holder — who wants to engage with the human layer of the Livepeer ecosystem. Not task-driven in the operational sense; motivated by connection, contribution, and staying informed.

**Why they land here:**
- Coming from Discord, forum, governance link, or internal referral
- Returning to find the right channel for their question
- Looking for contribution or involvement opportunities
- Staying current with ecosystem developments

**Three distinct intents:**

| Intent | Arrives with | Leaves with |
|---|---|---|
| **Intent A: Find the community** | "Where do I talk to people?" | Discord invite, forum category, confidence there is an active community |
| **Intent B: Follow what's happening** | "What's the latest? What are people discussing?" | Trending topics, active proposals, calendar link |
| **Intent C: Contribute** | "I want to do more. How do I get involved?" | SPE process, contribution taxonomy, funded pathways |

**Information needs:**
- Platform map with purpose per surface
- Discord channel guide for their role
- Contribution types taxonomy
- Funded opportunities inventory
- Governance participation surfaces
- Community tools and guides

**Voice preference:** Scannable, direct, outcome-forward. No hype language.

**Navigation pattern:**
1. Portal (orient — which platform?)
2. Channels or Ways to Contribute (navigate)
3. Opportunities or Governance Participation (activate)

### Rank #2: The Curious Newcomer

**Who they are:** Just heard about Livepeer. Doesn't know what role they'd play. Landing from homepage, social link, or referral. Evaluating whether to participate.

**Why they land here:**
- Wants to know if the community is active and welcoming
- Needs to understand how the community is structured
- Looking for a place to introduce themselves
- Forming first impression of the network

**What they need:**
- One-sentence explainer: what is this community?
- Evidence of activity (forum threads, call frequency, event calendar)
- Who's here — four roles, clear role descriptions
- Where to start as a newcomer
- Community norms and code of conduct
- One concrete entry point (usually Discord #landing-pad)

**Information needs:**
- Who's Here page (orient to roles and structure)
- Community Portal (entry point)
- Community Guidelines (confidence + expectations)
- Channels page (platform map, especially Discord)
- Community Calendar (evidence of activity)

**Voice preference:** Warm, welcoming, factual. Short prose, clear hierarchy.

**Navigation pattern:**
1. Community Portal (quick orientation)
2. Who's Here (understand the structure)
3. Channels — Discord section (find #landing-pad)

---

### Rank #3: The Governance Participant (Or Aspiring)

**Who they are:** Active or aspiring participant in protocol governance. Wants to vote, understand SPEs, draft a proposal, or participate in governance discussions.

**Why they land here:**
- Looking for governance process explainer
- Seeking SPE/workstream information
- Looking for GovWorks contact
- Scoping a proposal idea before drafting

**What they need:**
- How governance works (conceptual — mechanics in LPT tab)
- Where proposals live (Forum + Explorer)
- SPE process explained (5-phase)
- Workstream list and how to join
- GovWorks contact and mentorship pathway
- Examples of past proposals

**Information needs:**
- Governance Participation page (surfaces, process levels)
- Workstreams page (active list with Forum links)
- FAQ (SPE/workstream definitions)
- Cross-links to LPT tab (voting mechanics, treasury)

**Voice preference:** Factual, structured, outcome-forward. No hand-waving about "community-driven."

**Navigation pattern:**
1. Governance Participation (what you can do)
2. Workstreams (active execution areas)
3. [Cross-link] LPT tab → Treasury (SPE mechanics)

---

### Rank #4: The Active Delegator / LPT Holder

**Who they are:** Already staked LPT. Wants community connection, governance guidance, peer resources, and evidence that their stake is in good hands.

**Why they land here:**
- Looking for community governance discussions
- Seeking delegator-specific community channels
- Wanting to stay informed on active proposals affecting them
- Looking for peer evaluation tools or guides

**What they need:**
- Governance participation pathways (voting, discussion, proposal drafting)
- Active proposals pointer
- Live Pioneers link (delegator education)
- Delegator-specific Discord channels
- Community tools for orchestrator evaluation
- Live Pioneers app link for community connection

**Information needs:**
- Governance Participation page (voting surfaces)
- Channels page (Discord — #delegating channel)
- Community Tools (orchestrator comparison tools)
- Community Guides (delegator-focused content)
- Cross-links to LPT tab (voting, delegation mechanics)

**Voice preference:** Precision, financial awareness. Direct links to live sources.

**Navigation pattern:**
1. Channels page (find #delegating)
2. Governance Participation (voting surface)
3. Community Tools (orchestrator evaluation)

---

### Rank #5: The Potential Contributor (Non-Technical)

**Who they are:** Developer, writer, translator, community supporter wanting to contribute but unsure how or where to start. May not know what "contribution" looks like beyond code.

**Why they land here:**
- "What can I contribute that isn't code?"
- Seeking funded contribution opportunities
- Looking for workstream involvement
- Evaluating translation or documentation paths

**What they need:**
- Contribution taxonomy (six types: code, docs, governance, support, translation, ideas)
- Pathways per type with first concrete action
- Funded opportunities (grants, RFPs, workstreams)
- Recognition and visibility mechanisms
- Community tools and resources
- How to get connected to the right team

**Information needs:**
- Ways to Contribute page (all types, with CTAs)
- Opportunities page (funded pathways)
- Workstreams page (join paths)
- Community Guides (examples of contributions)
- Cross-links to Developers tab (code and grant pathways)

**Voice preference:** Practical, action-forward, outcome-first. No jargon.

**Navigation pattern:**
1. Ways to Contribute (all six types)
2. Opportunities (funded vs non-funded)
3. [If code] → Developers tab; [If governance] → Governance Participation; [If translation] → Live Pioneers

## SECONDARY PERSONAS (Not Seated But Served)

### Press / Researchers

**What they need:** Evidence of community health — activity levels, governance participation, event frequency, ecosystem maturity. Usually leaves quickly. Serve by:
- Clear metrics and links to live sources (Forum activity, Explorer governance record, Calendar)
- Who's Here page (shows organisational maturity)
- Governance Participation (demonstrates sophistication)

### Existing Orchestrators

**What they need:** Governance context, peer networks, community tools, operational intelligence. Served by:
- Channels page (community channels for orchestrators)
- Governance Participation (governance affecting their operation)
- Community Tools (dashboards and performance trackers)
