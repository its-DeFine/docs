## VOICE REGISTER

The Community tab has a different voice register than technical tabs. It is not instructional (that's a how-to page). It is not referential in the academic sense (that's a glossary). It is **invitational and directional** — it orients people and moves them toward action.

**Core voice characteristics:**

- **Outcome-forward** — every statement points toward what the reader can accomplish
- **Warm but factual** — welcoming without hype; honest about what exists
- **Active** — readers as agents doing things, not as recipients of information
- **Direct** — no hedging, no corporate language, plain English
- **Role-aware** — adjusts register slightly per audience (governance vs contribution vs newcomer)

**Core voice prohibitions:**

- No exclamation marks on factual content (e.g., "Livepeer is amazing!" ❌)
- No "thriving community," "passionate contributors," "amazing ecosystem" ✗
- No passive voice ("this page can help you...") — always active ("you can...", "the Forum is where...")
- No Web3 jargon without immediate plain-language context
- No corporate hedging ("helps ensure," "aims to") — use direct language instead

## VOICE BY CONTENT TYPE

### Orientation / Overview (Portal, Who's Here, FAQ)

**Register:** Warm, welcoming, factual, concise  
**Goal:** Build confidence that this is a real, active community; orient to next step

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Livepeer runs on the people who build, operate, delegate, and govern it. Each role is essential. Here's who they are."
- ✓ "The community is distributed across Discord, the Forum, Telegram, X, YouTube, and Luma. Each has a distinct purpose."
- ✗ "Livepeer has an amazing, passionate community!" (hype language)
- ✗ "The community can help you with many things." (passive, vague)

### Governance Explainers (Governance Participation, Workstreams, FAQ sections)

**Register:** Clear, factual, structured, civic  
**Goal:** Make the process transparent and executable; signal that governance is accessible

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Livepeer governance is on-chain, binding, and open to any staked LPT holder. You can read proposals, comment, vote, or draft a new one."
- ✓ "An SPE is a treasury proposal. The process has five phases: pre-proposal, discussion, temperature check, refinement, on-chain vote."
- ✗ "Governance is a complex process that enables community input." (vague, passive)
- ✗ "Our innovative governance system empowers stakeholders." (marketing language)

### Contribution Pathways (Ways to Contribute, Opportunities)

**Register:** Practical, action-forward, outcome-first  
**Goal:** Lower the barrier to action; make clear what "contribute" means

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Code: Open-source repos on GitHub. For large features, open a discussion first."
- ✓ "Documentation: Flag issues or submit a PR on the docs repo. First PR? Ask in #docs on Discord."
- ✓ "Governance: Comment on proposals in the Forum, vote in Explorer, or draft an SPE with GovWorks mentorship."
- ✗ "Code contribution is a great way to get involved in open source." (vague, hype)
- ✗ "Contributors are the lifeblood of the community." (cliché)

### Channel & Platform Directory (Channels, Events and Calls, News and Updates)

**Register:** Scannable, direct, no superlatives  
**Goal:** Help readers find the right surface quickly without sales language

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Discord — real-time discussion, support, governance coordination"
- ✓ "Forum — long-form discussion, governance proposals, technical debates"
- ✓ "Water Cooler Call — weekly, open to all; community discussion and wins"
- ✗ "Discord is where the magic happens" (cliché)
- ✗ "The amazing community team is always ready to help" (vague, hype)

### Community Guidelines

**Register:** Direct, inclusive, non-punitive  
**Goal:** Set clear expectations; signal that the space is professional and safe

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Be constructive. Assume good faith. Engage with ideas, not people."
- ✓ "Discord: use the right channel. Keep #general accessible for newcomers and questions."
- ✓ "If you see a violation, report it in #mods. We'll follow up within 48 hours."
- ✗ "Violations will not be tolerated." (punitive, threatening)
- ✗ "We pride ourselves on our inclusive community." (corporate)

## CONTENT STRUCTURE STANDARDS

### Headings
- Use a clear hierarchy (H2 for sections, H3 for subsections, H4 for details)
- Headings reveal the page structure — a reader should understand the page by reading headings only
- Use **active voice and noun phrases**: "Ways to Contribute" not "How You Can Contribute"

### Lists and Tables
- **Scannable priority:** Bulleted lists > numbered lists > prose paragraphs
- Use tables for comparison matrices (platform map, contribution types, workstream list)
- One concept per bullet; no periods if incomplete sentences
- Tables should have a clear header row and consistent column semantics

### Prose Sections
- **Short explainers:** 2–3 sentences max per concept
- Link to external sources rather than duplicating them (e.g., "For the full SPE process, see the LP Token tab")
- Use **bolded key terms** first introduction only (e.g., "**SPE** (Strategic Priority Execution) is...")
- Avoid nested parenthetical explanations; use dashes instead

### Links
- **All links are outcome-forward:** "Vote on Explorer" not "Visit Explorer"
- **Cross-tab links have context:** "For voting mechanics, see the LP Token tab → Governance"
- **External links are flagged:** "Forum" for internal community surface, explicit URL for external repos
- **Live source links are clearly marked:** "Subscribe to the Luma calendar" (indicates live data)

## VOICE BY PERSONA

### Newcomer-Facing Content (Portal, Who's Here, Community Guidelines, Channels)

- **Tone:** Warm, welcoming, orientation-first
- **Depth:** Surface-level; explain jargon; don't assume knowledge
- **Language:** Plain English; no protocol terms without definition
- **Example voice:** "The Forum is where the community has longer discussions. Use it for questions that need a detailed answer or that you want searchable."

### Governance Participant-Facing Content (Governance Participation, Workstreams, FAQ)

- **Tone:** Factual, structured, precise
- **Depth:** Process-level; mechanics are owned elsewhere
- **Language:** Governance terminology is acceptable here (proposal, SPE, quorum, etc.)
- **Example voice:** "The SPE process has five phases: pre-proposal on the Forum, 3-day discussion window, 3-day temperature check on-chain, 5-day refinement, 7-day voting window."

### Contributor-Facing Content (Ways to Contribute, Opportunities, Community Guides)

- **Tone:** Practical, action-forward, recognition-conscious
- **Depth:** Pathway-level; link to full mechanics
- **Language:** Mixed; use both plain English and skill-specific jargon (git, PR, LIP, etc.)
- **Example voice:** "Documentation: Submit a PR to the docs repo. First PR? Ask in #docs on Discord. Merged PRs are credited in the changelog."

### Evaluator-Facing Content (Who's Here, Governance Participation, Community Tools)

- **Tone:** Factual, data-oriented, honest
- **Depth:** Evidence-level; link to primary sources
- **Language:** Technical but accessible to non-Livepeer-native readers
- **Example voice:** "The Livepeer Foundation (launched June 2025) coordinates contributors and stewards long-term direction. It does not control the treasury — token holders do via governance votes."

## CONTENT REVIEW CHECKLIST

Before publishing any Community page:

- [ ] **Outcome-forward:** Does every section point toward what the reader can accomplish?
- [ ] **Plain language:** Would someone unfamiliar with Livepeer understand this?
- [ ] **No hype:** Are there any exclamation marks, adjectives like "amazing," or corporate language?
- [ ] **Active voice:** Are readers the subject of sentences ("You can vote") not objects ("Voting is available")?
- [ ] **Link quality:** Does each external reference have a URL and clear purpose?
- [ ] **Scannability:** Can a reader understand this page from headings alone?
- [ ] **Handoff clarity:** Are cross-tab references explicit and contextual?
- [ ] **No duplication:** Have I checked the LPT, Orchestrators, and Developers tabs for overlapping content?
- [ ] **Staleness signal:** If content changes quarterly, is there a `{/* REVIEW */}` comment and a link to the live source?
