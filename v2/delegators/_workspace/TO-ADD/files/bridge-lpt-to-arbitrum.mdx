Livepeer delegation runs on Arbitrum One. LPT on Ethereum mainnet or held on a centralised exchange must reach an Arbitrum One wallet before you can bond. This page covers both paths.

---

## Before you start

You need:
- A self-custody wallet (MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, or any WalletConnect-compatible wallet)
- The wallet configured for Arbitrum One — add the network at [chainlist.org](https://chainlist.org) if it does not appear
- ETH on Ethereum mainnet for bridge gas (typically $5–20 depending on conditions)
- A small ETH balance on Arbitrum One for future delegation transactions — under $1 is sufficient

---

## Path A — LPT in a wallet on Ethereum mainnet

Use the [Arbitrum Bridge](https://bridge.arbitrum.io) or the [Livepeer L2 Migration Tool](https://explorer.livepeer.org/migrate) if you have legacy LPT bonded on the L1 protocol.

<StyledSteps>

<StyledStep title="Go to bridge.arbitrum.io and connect your wallet">
  Navigate to [bridge.arbitrum.io](https://bridge.arbitrum.io). Connect the wallet holding your LPT on Ethereum mainnet.

  Select **Ethereum → Arbitrum One** as the direction. Select **LPT** as the token (search by name or contract address: `0x58b6A8A3302369DAEc383334672404Ee733aB239`).
</StyledStep>

<StyledStep title="Enter the amount and approve">
  Enter the amount of LPT to bridge. Review the estimated gas cost on Ethereum mainnet — this is the primary cost of the operation.

  Approve the bridge contract to spend your LPT, then confirm the deposit transaction. Both require gas on Ethereum mainnet.
</StyledStep>

<StyledStep title="Wait for the deposit to process">
  Arbitrum bridge deposits take approximately 10–30 minutes to finalise. The bridge UI shows progress. You do not need to take any action during this period.
</StyledStep>

<StyledStep title="Verify your LPT on Arbitrum One">
  After the deposit finalises, switch your wallet network to Arbitrum One. Your LPT balance should appear in the wallet and in the [Livepeer Explorer](https://explorer.livepeer.org).

  For on-chain verification: go to [arbiscan.io](https://arbiscan.io), enter your wallet address, and check the token balances tab.
</StyledStep>

</StyledSteps>

<Tip>
  The Livepeer L2 Migration Tool at [explorer.livepeer.org/migrate](https://explorer.livepeer.org/migrate) handles legacy LPT bonded in the L1 Livepeer protocol. Use it if you were a delegator on the original Ethereum mainnet deployment.
</Tip>

## Path B — LPT on a centralised exchange {#from-a-centralised-exchange}

Most centralised exchanges cannot bridge directly to Arbitrum — you must withdraw to a wallet first.

### Step 1 — Check which network your exchange supports for LPT withdrawals

Before withdrawing, confirm whether your exchange supports:

- **Arbitrum One withdrawals** — You can withdraw LPT directly to an Arbitrum One address. Skip bridging entirely.
- **Ethereum mainnet withdrawals only** — You receive LPT on Ethereum mainnet. Follow Path A above to bridge from there.

<Warning>
  Withdrawing to the wrong network means your tokens are inaccessible. Confirm the network your exchange sends LPT on before initiating a withdrawal. When in doubt, withdraw to Ethereum mainnet and bridge — it costs more in gas but is always safe.
</Warning>

### Step 2a — If your exchange supports Arbitrum withdrawals

1. Initiate a withdrawal from the exchange to your Arbitrum One wallet address
2. Confirm the destination network is **Arbitrum One** (not Ethereum, not other L2s)
3. Wait for the exchange's processing time — typically 10–60 minutes
4. Verify your LPT balance in [explorer.livepeer.org](https://explorer.livepeer.org) or [arbiscan.io](https://arbiscan.io)

### Step 2b — If your exchange only supports Ethereum mainnet withdrawals

1. Withdraw LPT from the exchange to an Ethereum mainnet wallet address
2. Ensure you have ETH on Ethereum mainnet for gas
3. Follow **Path A** above to bridge your LPT to Arbitrum One

### Getting ETH on Arbitrum for gas

After bridging LPT, you need a small ETH balance on Arbitrum One for delegation transactions. Options:

- Bridge ETH using [bridge.arbitrum.io](https://bridge.arbitrum.io) — select ETH as the token
- Withdraw ETH directly to Arbitrum from an exchange that supports it
- Use a fiat-to-Arbitrum on-ramp (available in some regions via Coinbase, Transak, and others)

Under $1 of ETH covers all delegation gas costs with room to spare.
