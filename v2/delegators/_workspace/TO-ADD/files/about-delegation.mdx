Bonding LPT attributes your stake to an orchestrator and entitles you to a share of their rewards. Your tokens do not go to the orchestrator — they are held by the Livepeer BondingManager contract on Arbitrum One and remain yours throughout. This page explains the mechanics before you commit.

---

## Delegation is not lending

When you bond LPT, the BondingManager contract takes custody of the tokens. The orchestrator you choose cannot access, move, or spend them. You remain the economic owner at all times.

What you are doing is attributing your stake weight to an orchestrator. That attribution:

- Increases their total bonded stake, which raises their proportional share of network rewards and their priority for routing video and AI jobs
- Entitles you to a cut of what they earn, determined by their `rewardCut` and `feeShare` parameters
- Gives your bonded balance voting weight in on-chain governance

You can move your attribution to a different orchestrator at any time without waiting — only full withdrawal requires an unbonding period.

---

## The dilution cost of not staking

Each protocol round, new LPT is minted and distributed proportionally to all bonded stake. If your LPT is unbonded, you receive nothing from that minting — but your share of total supply decreases as new tokens go to active stakers.

The erosion rate depends on the current inflation rate, which adjusts automatically each round:

- **Below 50% of LPT bonded** → inflation increases, incentivising more delegation
- **Above 50% bonded** → inflation decreases toward equilibrium

Holding LPT without bonding means watching your proportional ownership dilute round by round while active stakers accumulate a growing share. Bonding is not optional for anyone who intends to preserve their proportional stake in the network.

<Note>
  10% of each round's minted LPT is diverted to the Livepeer Community Treasury under LIP-92. The remaining 90% is distributed to orchestrators and their delegators. See [Delegation Economics](/v2/delegators/delegation/delegation-economics) for how this affects your returns.
</Note>

## What your tokens do while bonded

Bonded LPT sits in the BondingManager contract, attributed to your chosen orchestrator. Each round that your orchestrator calls the `Reward()` function on-chain:

1. New LPT is minted for that round's inflation
2. Your orchestrator retains their `rewardCut` percentage
3. The remainder is distributed proportionally across all stake bonded to that orchestrator — including yours
4. Your pending balance in the contract grows accordingly

Rewards accumulate in contract state. They do not arrive in your wallet automatically. You claim them separately — and the timing of when you claim relative to the `Reward()` call matters. See [Manage Your Delegation](/v2/delegators/delegation/manage-your-delegation#claim-timing) for the timing detail.

## What you can and cannot do while bonded

| Action | Allowed? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Redelegate to a different orchestrator | ✅ Yes | Takes effect next round; no unbonding required |
| Add more LPT to your position | ✅ Yes | Bond again from the same wallet |
| Claim accumulated earnings | ✅ Yes | Moves rewards into your bonded balance |
| Vote on governance proposals | ✅ Yes | Full bonded balance counts as voting weight |
| Partially unbond | ✅ Yes | A portion exits; the remainder stays bonded and earning |
| Withdraw immediately | ❌ No | Requires initiating unbonding, then waiting for the thawing period |
| Transfer bonded LPT to another address | ❌ No | Unbond and withdraw first |
| Bridge bonded LPT to Ethereum mainnet | ❌ No | Unbond and withdraw first |

---

## The thawing period

Withdrawing your LPT to your wallet is a two-step process:

1. **Initiate unbonding** — Your stake exits the bonded state and stops earning rewards from this point.
2. **Wait for the thawing period** — The protocol enforces a mandatory delay before your tokens become withdrawable. You can cancel and rebond during this window if you change your mind.
3. **Withdraw** — After the delay, your LPT moves from the contract to your wallet.

The thawing period exists so the protocol can apply any slashing penalties before stake is freed. Slashing is currently disabled on Livepeer mainnet — the mechanism exists but is not active.

<Info>
  The unbonding period is a governance-controlled parameter. See [Protocol Parameters](/v2/delegators/resources/reference/protocol-parameters) for the current value verified against on-chain state.
</Info>

To switch orchestrators rather than exit, use redelegation — it transfers your stake attribution without any thawing period.

---

## Safety

The primary risk in delegation is orchestrator underperformance, not token loss:

- **Slashing is disabled.** Bonded LPT cannot be reduced as a protocol penalty on the current mainnet.
- **The orchestrator cannot access your tokens.** The BondingManager enforces this at the contract level.
- **Missed `Reward()` calls mean zero income for that round.** No catch-up in subsequent rounds. The round's allocation is permanently forfeit for every delegator in that pool.
- **Commission can change without notice.** Orchestrators can update `rewardCut` and `feeShare` at any time, effective from the next round.

The [Choose an Orchestrator](/v2/delegators/delegation/choose-an-orchestrator) guide covers how to evaluate reward call reliability before you commit.
