## What delegation does

Livepeer uses a delegated proof-of-stake model. Orchestrators (GPU node operators) bond LPT to participate in the network. Their stake weight determines how much work they receive and how many inflationary rewards they earn each round. Delegators increase an orchestrator's stake, receiving a share of the orchestrator's earnings in return.

**Key facts:**
- Your LPT stays in the Livepeer BondingManager contract — non-custodial
- You can only delegate to one orchestrator at a time from a single wallet
- Exiting delegation requires an unbonding period before tokens are withdrawable — see [Protocol Parameters](/v2/delegators/resources/reference/protocol-parameters) for the current duration
- Unbonded LPT is diluted each round as active stakers receive newly minted LPT

<Note>
  If less than 50% of LPT is bonded, inflation increases to incentivise more delegation. If more than 50% is bonded, inflation decreases. Unbonded LPT holders receive none of this issuance.
</Note>

---

## What you earn

Delegators earn from two sources:

**Inflationary LPT** — New LPT is minted each round proportional to total bonded stake. Orchestrators keep their `rewardCut` percentage and distribute the rest to delegators proportionally.

**ETH fees** — Gateways pay orchestrators ETH for transcoding and AI inference jobs. Orchestrators keep a portion and distribute the rest to delegators based on their `feeShare` setting.

<Warning>
  **rewardCut and feeShare move in opposite directions.** Lower `rewardCut` is better for delegators (orchestrator keeps less inflation). Higher `feeShare` is better for delegators (orchestrator passes more ETH fees through). Read both before choosing an orchestrator. The Explorer labels `feeShare` as "Fee Share".
</Warning>

Returns depend on the orchestrator's commission settings, their reward call reliability, network usage levels, and the current inflation rate.

## Before you start

- LPT in an Ethereum-compatible wallet (MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, WalletConnect-compatible)
- ETH on Arbitrum One for gas — a few cents per transaction
- LPT on Arbitrum One (Livepeer staking runs on Arbitrum, not Ethereum mainnet)

## Step-by-step: how to delegate

<StyledSteps>
  <StyledStep title="Go to the Livepeer Explorer">
    Navigate to [explorer.livepeer.org](https://explorer.livepeer.org) and connect your wallet. The Explorer is the primary interface for delegation and reward claiming.
  </StyledStep>

  <StyledStep title="Choose an orchestrator">
    Browse the orchestrators list. Evaluate in this order:

    1. **Active status** — Only orchestrators in the top 100 by bonded stake earn rewards in the current round
    2. **Reward call history** — How consistently they call `Reward()` each round (most important signal)
    3. **Reward Cut** — The percentage of LPT inflation the orchestrator keeps. Lower means more for delegators.
    4. **Fee Share** — The percentage of ETH fees passed to delegators. Higher means more for delegators.
    5. **Delegated stake** — Higher gives them more network weight; higher concentration raises centralisation concerns

    <Tip>
      A low reward cut only matters if the orchestrator is actively earning. Check reward call history first — an orchestrator who misses rounds earns you nothing, regardless of their commission rate.
    </Tip>

    For a full evaluation framework, see [Choose an Orchestrator](/v2/delegators/delegation/choose-an-orchestrator).
  </StyledStep>

  <StyledStep title="Approve and bond LPT">
    On the orchestrator's profile page, click **Delegate** and enter the amount of LPT to bond. You sign two transactions:

    1. **Approve** — Authorises the BondingManager contract to transfer your LPT
    2. **Bond** — Locks your LPT and attributes your stake to the orchestrator

    Both cost a few cents in Arbitrum gas.
  </StyledStep>

  <StyledStep title="Monitor your position">
    After bonding, the Explorer shows your delegated position, your orchestrator's current stats, and your accumulated pending rewards.

    Rewards accumulate each round automatically. No action is required for rewards to accrue — but you must claim them to realise them.
  </StyledStep>

  <StyledStep title="Claim your earnings">
    Rewards accumulate in the BondingManager contract. To move them into your bonded balance (for compounding or withdrawal), call `claimEarnings` from your account page in the Explorer.

    <Warning>
      **Claim after your orchestrator calls `Reward()` for the round — not before.** If you claim before `Reward()` is called in a round, the contract records your `lastClaimRound` as the current round and you forfeit that round's rewards entirely. See [Manage Your Delegation](/v2/delegators/delegation/manage-your-delegation#claim-timing) for details.
    </Warning>
  </StyledStep>

  <StyledStep title="Unbond when you want to withdraw">
    To withdraw your LPT:

    1. Click **Undelegate** in the Explorer — initiates the unbonding lock
    2. Wait for the thawing period — see [Protocol Parameters](/v2/delegators/resources/reference/protocol-parameters) for the current duration
    3. Click **Withdraw** to return LPT to your wallet

    The thawing period exists to allow the protocol time to enforce any slashing penalties before stake is released.
  </StyledStep>
</StyledSteps>

## Risk factors

<BorderedBox variant="accent" padding="16px">

- **Operator underperformance** — If your orchestrator stops calling `Reward()`, you earn no LPT inflation. Choose operators with consistent history.
- **Slashing** — Currently disabled on Livepeer mainnet. The mechanism exists; future activation could penalise delegators for orchestrator misbehaviour.
- **Market volatility** — LPT and ETH prices fluctuate. USD returns depend on token prices beyond reward percentages.
- **Commission changes** — Orchestrators can update `rewardCut` and `feeShare` at any time with no notice. Monitor your position periodically.
- **Concentration risk** — Delegating to very large orchestrators increases centralisation risk. Distributing stake across the ecosystem is better for network health.
- **Unbonding delay** — Stake is locked during the thawing period and cannot be moved quickly in response to events.

</BorderedBox>
