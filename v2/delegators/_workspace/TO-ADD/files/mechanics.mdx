Delegation generates returns through a set of on-chain mechanisms that operate on a fixed time unit — the round. Understanding how rounds, reward calls, and reward accumulation interact tells you why your orchestrator's reliability matters as much as their commission rate.

---

## Rounds

A round is the Livepeer protocol's fundamental time unit. Each round covers approximately 5,760 Arbitrum blocks — roughly one day, though this varies with Arbitrum block production rates.

Round boundaries trigger:
- Active set election (which orchestrators earn this round)
- Inflation rate adjustment
- Reward distribution eligibility

The current round number and timing are visible on [explorer.livepeer.org](https://explorer.livepeer.org).

---

## The active set

At the start of each round, the protocol selects the top orchestrators by total bonded stake to form the active set — the only operators eligible to receive transcoding and AI inference jobs and earn inflation rewards that round.

Orchestrators outside the active set earn nothing, regardless of uptime or commission settings. As a delegator, your stake only earns if your orchestrator holds an active set position.

<Info>
  The active set size is a governance-controlled protocol parameter. See [Protocol Parameters](/v2/delegators/resources/reference/protocol-parameters) for the current value verified against the BondingManager contract.
</Info>

---

## The Reward() call

Each round, active orchestrators must call the `Reward()` function on-chain. This call:

1. Triggers the BondingManager to calculate that round's inflation allocation for the orchestrator's pool
2. Mints new LPT via the Minter contract
3. Distributes the minted LPT across all bonded stake in the pool, after the orchestrator retains their `rewardCut`

**If an orchestrator does not call `Reward()` in a round, no LPT is minted for that pool.** The allocation is not deferred to the next round — it is permanently forfeit for every delegator in that pool.

Reward calls require a small ETH balance to cover Arbitrum gas. Operators who let their ETH run to zero stop calling until they top up. This is the primary cause of missed reward calls.

---

## The BondingManager

The BondingManager is the central Livepeer smart contract managing all bonding, unbonding, reward accounting, and earnings. When you delegate:

- Your LPT is transferred into the BondingManager contract
- Your stake is attributed to your chosen orchestrator
- Your pending balance grows each round your orchestrator calls `Reward()`

The BondingManager tracks earnings using a **cumulative reward factor**: a running multiplier that grows each round when `Reward()` is called. This design means the contract does not need to update every delegator's state every round — it calculates each delegator's accumulated earnings on demand when they claim, using the difference between the factor at their last claim and the current factor.

The result is automatic compounding: as your pending rewards grow and accrue into your bonded balance (when you claim), the growing balance earns on a progressively larger base.

---

## claimEarnings()

`claimEarnings()` is the on-chain function that moves accumulated rewards into your bonded balance. You can call it directly or it is triggered automatically by the following actions:

| Action | claimEarnings() called automatically |
|---|---|
| Adding to your bond (bond again) | ✅ Before updating amount |
| Unbonding | ✅ Before reducing amount |
| Rebonding | ✅ Before increasing amount |
| Redelegating to new orchestrator | ✅ Before transfer |
| Explicit claim via Explorer | ✅ Direct call |

### ⚠️ Claim timing — critical protocol behaviour

When `claimEarnings()` executes, the BondingManager records your `lastClaimRound` as the **current round at the time of the call**.

**If your orchestrator has not yet called `Reward()` for the current round at the moment you claim, you forfeit that round's rewards and fees entirely.** The contract will not retroactively credit you for that round — your `lastClaimRound` has already advanced past it.

This means:

- **Claim at the end of rounds** — after your orchestrator has already called `Reward()` for that round
- **Do not claim at the start of a round** — before your orchestrator has acted

This behaviour is confirmed by Livepeer Foundation Engineering (Discord, April 2026). The Livepeer Explorer is implementing a UI warning (PR #613) to flag this timing risk. Check the Explorer for the current state of this indicator before claiming.

Since `claimEarnings()` is also triggered automatically by bond, unbond, rebond, and redelegate actions, the same timing risk applies when performing any of those operations at the start of a round.

---

## Checking pending rewards

Two BondingManager functions return your current position without claiming:

- **`pendingStake(address, endRound)`** — Total bonded stake including unclaimed rewards
- **`pendingFees(address, endRound)`** — Pending ETH fees not yet claimed

Both are readable via [Arbiscan](https://arbiscan.io) by calling the BondingManager contract directly, or surfaced through the Explorer UI on your account page.

---

## Governance checkpoints

The BondingVotes contract (ERC-5805) records a snapshot of each delegator's bonded stake at the end of each round. These snapshots are used to calculate voting power for governance proposals — your vote weight is your bonded balance at the block when the proposal was created, not your current balance.

This prevents stake manipulation immediately before votes. It also means your governance weight is calculated from your historical position, so a recent large bond increase may not immediately affect your voting power on proposals created before that bond.
