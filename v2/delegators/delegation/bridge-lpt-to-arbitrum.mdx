## Overview

The Livepeer protocol runs entirely on Arbitrum One. Staking, delegation, rewards, and governance all require LPT on the Arbitrum One L2 chains.

LPT exists on both chains as separate token instances, bridged by Livepeer's custom bridge 'gateway' contracts.

The main ways to bridge between the Ethereum & Arbitrum chains are:

**Deposits: Ethereum LPT → Arbitrum One LPT**

| Route | Starting point | Speed | Web3 Skill |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bridge** | You hold LPT on Ethereum Mainnet already | 15–30 min | Low-Mid |
| **CEX** | You are buying LPT fresh, or already hold it on Binance/OKX | Minutes | Low |
| **DEX swap** | You are already on Arbitrum One and hold ETH, WETH, or USDC | Seconds | Mid-High |
| **Wallet swap** | Same as DEX, but within MetaMask, Ledger Live, or another wallet's built-in interface | Seconds | Mid-High |

**Withdrawals: Arbitrum One LPT → Ethereum LPT**

| Route | Best for | Speed | Web3 Skill |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bridge** | Trustless canonical path back to Ethereum Mainnet | 7-day challenge period | Mid |
| **CEX** | Moving LPT back to Ethereum without the 7-day wait | Minutes | Low |
| **DEX swap** | Exiting LPT into ETH or USDC while staying on Arbitrum One | Seconds | Mid-High |
| **Wallet swap** | Same as DEX, within your wallet's built-in interface | Seconds | Mid-High |

<Info>
  The bridge is the only trustless path. CEX, DEX, and wallet swap routes involve a counterparty - an exchange, a liquidity pool, or an aggregator — and carry counterparty risk: temporary custody of your tokens, slippage on DEX swaps, and withdrawal limits on CEX routes. For large amounts, the bridge is the safest option despite the 7-day wait on withdrawals.
</Info>

## Deposits: Ethereum LPT → Arbitrum One LPT

**Quick Guide Map**

| Route | Best for | Speed | Web3 Skill |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Official bridge** | Moving LPT you already hold on Ethereum Mainnet | 15–30 min | Low-Mid |
| **CEX (Binance / OKX)** | Buying LPT fresh, or moving between networks without the 7-day wait | Minutes | Low |
| **DEX swap (Uniswap V3)** | Already on Arbitrum One with ETH or USDC and want LPT instantly | Seconds | Mid-High |
| **Wallet swap** | Same as DEX — within MetaMask, Ledger Live, Trust Wallet etc. | Seconds | Mid-High |

<BorderedBox variant="accent">
<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Bridge" icon="cubes">

Deposits via the official Arbitrum bridge lock your LPT in `L1Escrow` on Ethereum and mint an equivalent amount on Arbitrum One via `BridgeMinter`. Deposits typically arrive within 15–30 minutes.

<Warning>
  Third-party bridges (Hop, Across, Jumper, Stargate) do not currently support LPT. LPT uses custom gateway contracts (`L1LPTGateway` / `L2LPTGateway`). Third-party bridges target the standard Arbitrum ERC-20 gateway, which LPT does not use.
</Warning>

## Withdrawals: Arbitrum One LPT → Ethereum LPT

**Quick Guide Map**

| Route | Best for | Speed | Web3 Skill |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Official bridge** | Moving LPT back to Ethereum Mainnet trustlessly | 7-day challenge period | Mid |
| **CEX (Binance / OKX)** | Avoiding the 7-day wait — deposit ARB LPT, withdraw ETH LPT | Minutes | Low |
| **DEX swap (Uniswap V3)** | Exiting LPT into ETH or USDC while staying on Arbitrum One | Seconds | Mid-High |
| **Wallet swap** | Same as DEX — within MetaMask, Ledger Live, Trust Wallet etc. | Seconds | Mid-High |

<BorderedBox variant="accent">
<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Bridge" icon="cubes">

Withdrawals via the bridge require an extra manual approval step. LPT on Arbitrum uses a custom gateway — the bridge UI does not handle approvals automatically for it. Approve the `L2LPTGateway` contract to spend your LPT on Arbitrum before the bridge UI will process the withdrawal.

<Warning>
  Withdrawals from Arbitrum One to Ethereum Mainnet are subject to Arbitrum's 7-day challenge period. Your LPT remains locked in transit until the 7 days elapse. Start a withdrawal at least 8 days before you need the LPT available on Ethereum.
</Warning>

<StyledSteps>
  <StyledStep title="Approve the L2LPTGateway on Arbiscan">
    Before using the bridge UI, you must manually approve the `L2LPTGateway` contract to spend your LPT. Open the <a href={`${arb}${lptL2}#writeContract`} target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">LPT write contract on Arbiscan</a> and connect your wallet.

    Scroll to the **approve** function and enter:

    - **Spender address**: <code>{l2Gateway}</code> (L2LPTGateway)
    - **Amount**: your LPT amount in wei (18 decimal places)

    Use a unit converter if needed: 1 LPT = `1000000000000000000` wei. For example, 100 LPT = `100000000000000000000`.

    Click **Write** and confirm the transaction in your wallet.

    <Info>
      This approval step exists because LPT uses a custom gateway. The standard Arbitrum bridge UI handles approvals automatically for tokens on the standard gateway, but LPT's custom gateway requires this manual step.
    </Info>
  </StyledStep>
  <StyledStep title="Connect to Arbitrum One on the bridge">
    Open [bridge.arbitrum.io](https://bridge.arbitrum.io/) and connect your wallet. Make sure it is set to **Arbitrum One** as the source network.
  </StyledStep>
  <StyledStep title="Select LPT and set Ethereum as destination">
    Select LPT from the token list. If it does not appear, paste the Arbitrum One LPT address:

    <code>{lptL2}</code>

    Set the destination to **Ethereum Mainnet** and enter the amount to withdraw.
  </StyledStep>
  <StyledStep title="Initiate the withdrawal">
    Click **Move funds** and confirm the transaction. This initiates the withdrawal on Arbitrum One.
  </StyledStep>
  <StyledStep title="Wait out the 7-day challenge period">
    After the transaction confirms on Arbitrum, a 7-day challenge period begins. This is a security property of Arbitrum's [optimistic rollup](/v2/about/resources/compendium/livepeer-glossary#optimistic-rollup) design and cannot be shortened. You will see a countdown in the **Transaction History** tab on the bridge.
  </StyledStep>
  <StyledStep title="Claim your LPT on Ethereum">
    Once the 7-day period elapses, switch your wallet to **Ethereum Mainnet**, return to [bridge.arbitrum.io](https://bridge.arbitrum.io/), open **Transaction History**, and click **Claim** next to your completed withdrawal. Confirm the transaction to receive your LPT on Ethereum Mainnet.
  </StyledStep>
</StyledSteps>

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="CEX" icon="money-bill-transfer">

  <StyledStep title="Send your Arbitrum One LPT to the exchange">

  </StyledStep>

## Troubleshooting

### Failed deposits

Deposit transactions occasionally fail when gas prices spike between Ethereum submission and Arbitrum sequencing. LPT that has not arrived after an hour likely has a failed cross-chain message — check its status below.

<StyledSteps>
  <StyledStep title="Check the retryable dashboard">
    Go to the [Arbitrum crosschain message dashboard](https://retryable-dashboard.arbitrum.io/) and paste your Ethereum deposit transaction hash. The dashboard will show whether your [retryable ticket](/v2/about/resources/compendium/livepeer-glossary#retryable-ticket) is pending, failed, or expired.
  </StyledStep>
  <StyledStep title="Resubmit if failed">
    For a failed ticket: connect your wallet and click to resubmit. This retries the L2 execution using current gas prices.

    <Warning>
      Act promptly. Retryable tickets expire after 7 days. A ticket that expires without being redeemed forfeits the ETH allocated to the L2 execution gas. The LPT itself remains locked in `L1Escrow` on Ethereum and is safe — contact Livepeer support to initiate recovery.
    </Warning>
  </StyledStep>
</StyledSteps>
