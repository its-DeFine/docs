It serves as a high-level overview and introduction to the different roles and responsibilities of the participants in the Livepeer protocol, and how they interact with each other and the protocol.

It provides a mental model for understanding the different actors in the system, their incentives, and how they contribute to the overall functioning of the Livepeer network.

There are only a few core actors in the Livepeer protocol
- Orchestrators
- Gateways: aka Broadcasters, the entities that submit video and AI jobs to the network
- Transcoders / AI Workers: the entities that perform the actual compute work of transcoding and AI inference
- Verifiers: the entities that verify the correctness of the work performed by the transcoders (BUT NOT AI workers), and ensure the integrity of the network
- Delegators (?): secure the network and earn rewards by bonding their stake

Define: role, interactions, incentives, responsibilities, how they relate to the protocol and network layers, and how they contribute to the overall functioning of the Livepeer ecosystem.

The protocol defines three core actors, and implements mechanisms to coordinate their interactions and align incentives for the desired network outcomes:
- **Orchestrators** - GPU nodes that perform transcoding and AI inference work, earn fees and staking rewards, and participate in governance
- **Gateways** - aka Broadcasters, the entities that submit video and AI jobs to the network, route work, manage payment flows, and connect to orchestrators
- **Delegators** - LPT token holders who stake to an orchestrator to secure the network and earn rewards, and participate in governance and voting

A fourth actor could be argued: **Transcoders** and **AI Workers** - specialised nodes that execute video transcoding and AI inference tasks in Docker containers or as external endpoints, and contribute to the overall Video and AI capabilities of the network.
