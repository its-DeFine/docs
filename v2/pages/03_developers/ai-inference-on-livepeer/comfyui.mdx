# ComfyUI – Full Global Model Support (Not Livepeer-Specific)

This page answers **one question only**:

> **What models can ComfyUI run, in principle, regardless of Livepeer support?**

It lists **all known model families commonly used with ComfyUI**, with a clear flag indicating **whether and why each may or may not be runnable on Livepeer**.

Nothing here implies Livepeer support.

---

## Legend

* ✅ **Likely runnable on Livepeer** (fits real-time / GPU-worker constraints)
* ⚠️ **Conditionally runnable** (depends on latency, VRAM, orchestration, or batching)
* ❌ **Not suitable for Livepeer** (design mismatch, stateful, CPU-bound, or non-deterministic)

---

## 1. Diffusion Models (Image / Video)

### Stable Diffusion Family

* Stable Diffusion 1.4 / 1.5 / 2.0 / 2.1 — ✅
  *Text-to-image and image-to-image diffusion models that generate images by iterative denoising; foundation of most ComfyUI workflows, including frame-by-frame video processing.*
* SDXL (Base / Refiner / Turbo) — ⚠️ (VRAM + latency)
  *Higher-capacity Stable Diffusion variant with improved composition and detail; Turbo variants trade quality for speed.*
* SD-Turbo / LCM / Lightning — ✅
  *Accelerated diffusion variants designed to converge in very few steps, enabling low-latency generation.*
* Kandinsky — ⚠️ (less optimized, heavier)
  *Diffusion-based image generation model emphasizing semantic understanding and text–image alignment.*
* DeepFloyd IF — ❌ (multi-stage, extreme VRAM)
  *Cascaded multi-stage diffusion system using large frozen text encoders for very high-fidelity images.*

**Why blocked (if any):** VRAM pressure, multi-stage graphs, inference latency

---

### Video Diffusion Models

* StreamDiffusion — ✅
  *Real-time diffusion architecture that reuses latent states across frames, enabling live video generation.*
* Stable Video Diffusion (SVD) — ⚠️ (latency, frame buffering)
  *Video diffusion model that generates short coherent clips by modeling temporal consistency across frames.*
* AnimateDiff — ⚠️ (temporal batching)
  *Adds motion modules to Stable Diffusion to create animated sequences from text or images.*
* VideoCrafter — ❌ (offline batch generation)
  *Offline video generation model designed for batch synthesis rather than interactive use.*
* Wan / Wan 2.x — ⚠️ (VRAM, unclear real-time guarantees)
  *Emerging video and world-style diffusion models that generate coherent scenes over time.*
* Gen-2–style world models — ❌ (stateful, non-deterministic)
  *Stateful world-generation models that maintain long-term scene memory and internal simulation state.*

**Why blocked:** temporal state, batch-only execution, non-real-time

---

## 2. Control & Conditioning Models

### ControlNet

* Canny — ✅
  *Edge-detection conditioning that guides diffusion using detected outlines.*
* Depth — ✅
  *Uses estimated depth maps to guide structure and spatial consistency in generated images.*
* OpenPose — ✅
  *Extracts human body, hand, and facial keypoints from images or video.*
  *Conditions diffusion on human skeletal keypoints for pose-aware generation.*
* Normal / Lineart / Scribble — ✅
  *Structural ControlNets that guide diffusion using surface normals, line drawings, or sketches.*
* Tile / Inpaint — ⚠️ (memory)
  *Enable tiled generation or region-specific editing to handle large images or partial modifications.*
* Multi-ControlNet compositions — ⚠️ (GPU saturation)
  *Combine multiple ControlNets simultaneously to enforce several structural constraints.*

### T2I / I2I Adapters

* IP-Adapter — ✅
  *Injects image-based style or identity guidance into diffusion models without retraining.*
* Reference-only adapters — ✅
  *Lightweight adapters that bias generation toward a reference image.*
* Style adapters — ✅
  *Adapters specialized for transferring artistic or visual style.*

---

## 3. Encoders, VAEs, and Latents

* SD VAEs — ✅
  *Standard variational autoencoders used to encode/decode images into latent space.*
* Custom / fine-tuned VAEs — ⚠️ (memory)
  *Non-standard VAEs that can improve quality but increase VRAM usage.*
* Latent consistency decoders — ✅
  *Decoders optimized for fewer diffusion steps and faster convergence.*

---

## 4. Vision Models (Non-Diffusion)

### Detection / Segmentation

* YOLO (v5–v8) — ⚠️ (CPU/GPU mix)
  *Real-time object detection models that identify and localize objects in images or video.*
* SAM / SAM2 — ⚠️ (VRAM, latency)
  *Segmentation models that generate pixel-level masks for objects or regions.*
* GroundingDINO — ⚠️
  *Vision–language detection model that links text prompts to detected objects.*
* Detectron2 — ❌ (CPU-heavy, batch-oriented)
  *Research framework for detection and segmentation, optimized for offline or batch workloads.*

### Depth / Geometry

* MiDaS — ✅
  *Monocular depth estimation model inferring depth from a single image.*
* DepthAnything — ✅
  *High-quality depth estimation model optimized for speed and accuracy.*
* ZoeDepth — ⚠️
  *Depth estimation model focused on higher precision at increased compute cost.*

---

## 5. Face, Pose & Human Models

* OpenPose — ✅
* DensePose — ⚠️ (heavy)
  *Maps image pixels to a 3D surface representation of the human body.*
* Face parsing — ⚠️
  *Segments facial regions such as eyes, mouth, hair, and skin.*
* FaceSwap / ReFace models — ❌ (legal + state)
  *Replace or modify faces in images or video, often involving identity and legal constraints.*
* Motion transfer (First Order Motion Model) — ❌ (temporal state)
  *Transfers motion from a driving video to a source subject using temporal state.*

---

## 6. Audio & Music Models

* Audio diffusion (Riffusion-style) — ⚠️
  *Generates audio or spectrograms using diffusion techniques.*
* MusicGen — ❌ (long-running, batch)
  *Text-to-music generation model producing full musical clips.*
* Bark / TTS models — ❌ (stateful, streaming mismatch)
  *Text-to-speech and voice generation models with long context windows.*

**Why blocked:** long context windows, non-frame-based execution

---

## 7. Multimodal & VLMs

* CLIP — ✅
  *Joint image–text embedding model used for scoring, ranking, and guidance.*
* BLIP / BLIP-2 — ⚠️
  *Vision–language models for image captioning and visual question answering.*
* LLaVA — ❌ (LLM state + latency)
  *Multimodal model combining vision encoders with large language models for reasoning.*
* Qwen-VL — ❌
  *Vision–language model integrating image understanding with LLM reasoning.*

---

## 8. LLMs (Text-Centric)

* LLaMA / Mistral / Qwen — ❌
  *Autoregressive large language models for text generation and reasoning.*
* GPT-style decoders — ❌
  *Transformer-based text generation models requiring token-by-token state.*

**Why blocked:** token streaming, memory residency, orchestration mismatch

---

## 9. 3D / NeRF / World Models

* NeRF — ❌ (offline training)
  *3D scene reconstruction models that learn continuous radiance fields from images.*
* Gaussian Splatting — ❌ (stateful scenes)
  *3D rendering technique using learned Gaussian primitives to represent scenes.*
* DreamFusion — ❌ (multi-hour jobs)
  *Uses diffusion guidance to optimize 3D representations over long-running jobs.*

---

## 10. Utility / Pre/Post Models

* ESRGAN / Real-ESRGAN — ⚠️
  *Image super-resolution models that upscale images while preserving detail.*
* GFPGAN — ⚠️
  *Face restoration model that enhances facial detail in low-quality images.*
* Background removal (U2Net) — ⚠️
  *Salient object detection model used to remove or isolate backgrounds.*

---

## Core Takeaway

ComfyUI can orchestrate **almost any PyTorch model**, but:

* Livepeer favors **stateless, frame-based, deterministic inference**
* Long-running, stateful, or batch-only models are **fundamentally incompatible**
* Real-time video imposes **hard physics limits**, not software ones

This list is exhaustive at the **model-family level** and intentionally conservative.
