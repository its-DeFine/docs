## Executive Summary

The Livepeer Treasury is a governance-controlled pool of LPT that funds ecosystem development, public goods, and protocol improvements. Treasury assets are controlled by on-chain governance on Arbitrum One.

Treasury balance after allocation:

\[
T' = T - A_i
\]

Where \(A_i\) represents the allocated amount for proposal \(i\).

    Governance --> Treasury --> Recipient

## Background

In 2022, Livepeer launched the On-Chain Treasury to fund the long-term growth and sustainability of the network. This treasury accumulates a portion of protocol inflation and is controlled by token holder votes.

By Q4 2022, [Messari reported](https://messari.io/asset/livepeer-protocol/whitepaper#on-chain-treasury) that Livepeer was allocating approximately $250k per quarter in grants to ecosystem projects via this governance mechanism.

By 2025, the treasury has become a significant source of funding for Special Purpose Entities (SPEs) and other needed initiatives.

---

## Community Treasury

The protocol routes a share of inflation to an on-chain community treasury. In its first iteration, 10% of newly minted LPT flowed into a multisig controlled by the Livepeer Foundation and community stewards. The purpose of the treasury is to fund public goods, research, marketing initiatives, and tooling that benefit the entire ecosystem.

Examples of funded initiatives include:

- Grants for improving monitoring infrastructure
- Research into verifiable transcoding
- Support for ecosystem builders

When the treasury balance reached a pre-defined cap, contributions paused; future LIPs can adjust the rate or resume funding.

---

## Governance and Accountability

Treasury funds do not accrue automatically to orchestrators or delegators. Spending proposals must be approved by governance, ensuring transparency and accountability.

Special-purpose entities (SPEs) can request allocations to execute scoped projects and must report back on milestones. Examples include:

- Building a verification framework
- Developing new codecs
- Infrastructure improvements

This structure turns inflation into a community-directed investment in the protocol's long-term health rather than pure dilution.

---

## Funding Frameworks

To ensure that treasury spending aligns with protocol objectives, the Livepeer community has experimented with several frameworks for public-goods funding:

| Framework | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Milestone-based grants** | Proposers submit budgets and deliverables; funds are released in tranches upon completion; progress is publicly reported on the forum |
| **Quadratic funding** | Community donations are matched from the treasury to signal strong grassroots support |
| **Retroactive funding** | Contributions are rewarded after impact is demonstrated |

These experiments reflect a wider movement in decentralized governance toward more inclusive and accountable resource allocation.

---

## Protocol vs Network Separation

**Protocol (On-Chain):**
- Treasury contract holds funds
- Governance proposals allocate funds
- On-chain voting determines outcomes

**Network (Off-Chain):**
- SPEs execute funded projects
- Grantees deliver milestones
- Community reports progress
