## The question driving the entire RESOURCES tab

> "I've encountered a term, changelog entry, or need for guidance -- where do I find the authoritative reference I need, and how do I know it's current and trustworthy?"

---

## How this was derived

RESOURCES serves a fundamentally different arriving situation than role tabs. Readers don't arrive seeking to understand a role or complete a workflow. They arrive **already in another tab** with a specific reference need:

- **Glossary lookup**: "What does 'orchestrator' mean in Livepeer's context?"
- **Change tracking**: "What's new in this month's go-livepeer release?"
- **Docs navigation**: "How do I contribute to these docs?"
- **Help**: "I'm stuck. Where do I find answers?"

Unlike other tabs, RESOURCES readers don't "arrive to RESOURCES." They arrive to their origin tab (role tab) **and find they need a reference**. The navigation choice "go to Resources" is made **mid-journey in another tab**.

## Per-persona variants

| Persona | Their specific question | Entry vector |
|---|---|---|
| **Cross-Tab Reference Seeker** | "What does this term mean?" / "What changed in the latest release?" | Sidebar link from role tab, internal CTA |
| **Protocol Researcher** | "Where is the authoritative spec for this mechanism?" | Web search, external link, blog reference |
| **Governance Participant** | "Where's the LIP index? What's the proposal status?" | Forum, Discord governance channel |
| **New Arrival** | "I'm new, how do I use this site?" | Homepage link, random search landing |
