#### Who's Here
**Purpose:** orient | **pageType:** concept | **Voice:** narrative, concise

**Name:** Who's Here

**Description:** The Livepeer community — who participates, why they're here, and how the network depends on each of them.

**Introduction:**  
Livepeer is a network, not a product. Its operators, builders, delegators, and contributors are not users — they are the network's infrastructure, governance, and growth engine.

**Required sections:**

**Four Roles**  
Developers build on the network via APIs, SDKs, and gateway integrations. Orchestrators contribute GPU compute and earn ETH fees and LPT rewards. Delegators stake LPT to secure the network and share in rewards. Contributors — governance participants, builders, writers, translators — shape the ecosystem's direction and tooling. Each role links to its primary tab.

**Why It's a Community**  
In a decentralised network, decisions, tooling, and direction emerge from participants — not a central team. Governance votes move real funds. Orchestrator economics shift with delegation choices. Contribution directly changes the product. This is the connective tissue between those roles.

**The Livepeer Foundation**  
Launched June 2025. Non-profit, coordinates contributors, stewards long-term direction, distinct from Livepeer Inc. The Foundation does not control the network — token holders do. For governance mechanics and the full organisational structure, see the About tab and the Home tab's ecosystem page.

#### Community Guidelines
**Purpose:** reference | **pageType:** reference | **Voice:** direct, plain

**Name:** Community Guidelines

**Description:** Conduct standards and contribution norms for every Livepeer community space — Discord, Forum, GitHub, Telegram, events, and anywhere the Livepeer community gathers.

**Introduction:**  
These guidelines keep every Livepeer space — from Discord to in-person events — constructive, open, and safe for contributors at every level. They apply everywhere.

**Required sections:**

**Core Principles**  
Be constructive. Assume good faith. Engage with ideas, not people. Contribute to the conversation even when you disagree. Four principles in plain language — no legal register, no corporate hedging.

**Platform-Specific Norms**  
Discord: use the right channel, keep #general accessible for newcomers. Forum: well-formed topics in the right category, no cross-posting. GitHub: complete issue templates, respect reviewer time. Telegram: quick questions and updates. One expectation per platform, stated directly.

**Contribution Standards**  
Code: open a discussion before large features. Documentation: one fix per PR, cite sources. Governance: engage on Forum before moving to on-chain vote. Community support: lead with what you have tried. Bug reports: full reproduction steps.

**Enforcement**  
Who handles violations. How to report. What happens. Proportionate and matter-of-fact — not a threat list.

#### Governance Participation
**Purpose:** orient + activate | **pageType:** concept | **Voice:** factual, outcome-forward

**Name:** Governance Participation

**Description:** How community members participate in shaping the Livepeer network — voting, proposing, and engaging with the governance process.

**Introduction:**  
Livepeer governance is on-chain, binding, and open to any staked LPT holder. This page maps the participation surfaces — where to vote, where to discuss, and where to get support for a proposal idea.

**Required sections:**

**What You Can Do**  
Four participation levels with a concrete action and surface for each: Read active proposals (Forum governance category + Explorer). Comment and give feedback (Forum threads before a vote). Vote on-chain (Explorer — any staked LPT). Draft and propose (Forum pre-proposal → GovWorks mentorship → on-chain submission). No mechanics detail here — the LPT tab has the full step-by-step.

**Where Governance Happens**  
Two surfaces: the Forum (off-chain discussion, proposals, pre-proposals) and the Explorer (on-chain voting, treasury, live proposals). These are the two surfaces every governance participant needs bookmarked. Links to both, one-sentence purpose each.

**GovWorks**  
The community's meta-governance SPE, run by StableLab. Supports proposal authors through the process, publishes governance digests, maintains the Livepeer Governance Hub. Contact via the GovWorks Notion Hub. If you have an idea for an SPE or protocol improvement, this is the first call.

**Go Deeper**  
For full delegation mechanics, voting how-to, and the governance model: LP Token tab → Governance section. For the on-chain governance architecture: About tab → Governance Model.

#### Ways to Contribute
**Purpose:** orient + activate | **pageType:** landing | **Voice:** concise, invitational

**Name:** Ways to Contribute

**Description:** Every way to contribute to the Livepeer ecosystem — from code and documentation to governance, community support, and translation.

**Introduction:**  
Livepeer is open source and community-governed. Useful participation doesn't require writing code — and some of the highest-value contributions are governance, support, and translation.

**Required sections:**

**Contribution Types**  
Six types, each with a one-sentence description and a concrete first action:  
— Code: open-source repos on GitHub, discussion-first for large features  
— Documentation: flag issues or submit a PR on the docs repo  
— Governance: comment on proposals, vote, or draft an SPE  
— Community Support: answer questions in Discord or the Forum  
— Translation: join multilingual community groups via Live Pioneers  
— Ideas and Feedback: post on the Forum — community thinking feeds the product

**For Builders and Developers**  
Funded pathways — grants, RFPs, hackathons, bug bounties — are documented in the Developers tab → Builder Opportunities. That's the canonical destination for funded technical contribution.

**Live Pioneers**  
An independent community initiative producing multilingual delegator education, ecosystem recaps, and Know Your Orchestrator interviews. Funded through treasury grants. Operates in 8+ languages. For multilingual participants, this is the fastest on-ramp to an active community group. Link to livepioneers.app.

Table: Platform · Purpose · Best for · Link.

**Discord Channel Guide**  
Key channels with one-sentence purpose:

**Discord vs Forum**  
Discord for real-time, conversational questions. Forum for questions that need a permanent, searchable answer — technical depth, governance pre-proposals, protocol discussion. When in doubt: Forum lasts; Discord disappears.
