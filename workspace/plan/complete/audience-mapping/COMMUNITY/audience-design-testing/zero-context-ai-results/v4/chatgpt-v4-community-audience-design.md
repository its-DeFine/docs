## Ideal Journey

**Linear**:

| Position | Stage name | lifecycleStage | What they're doing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify your participation lane | discover | Working out whether they are here to observe, contribute, seek funding, participate in governance, or map the ecosystem |
| 2 | Build a mental model of how the ecosystem works | discover | Learning how discussion, strategy, funding, execution, and accountability fit together |
| 3 | Join the places where activity actually happens | setup | Entering the right channels, calls, and forums for their path |
| 4 | Choose a concrete way to participate | evaluate | Selecting a contribution, governance, funding, or ecosystem-following path |
| 5 | Take the first visible action | build | Posting, joining, contributing, responding to an RFP, or engaging in a live thread |
| 6 | Learn the governance and funding flow | build | Understanding how ideas move from discussion to decision to execution |
| 7 | Participate repeatedly with context | operate | Returning for proposals, accountability, calls, and community coordination |
| 8 | Graduate into specialist depth where needed | operate | Moving into role-specific or technical tabs without losing community context |

**On-demand**:
- Current calls, recurring events, and where they happen
- Active proposals, pre-proposals, and accountability discussions
- The difference between Foundation, Treasury, Advisory Boards, Workstreams, and SPEs
- Funding path fit: grant vs bounty vs RFP vs treasury proposal
- Public venues for discussion: Forum categories, Discord channels, event threads
- Ecosystem project and team map
- Live tracking surfaces such as Explorer and treasury views
- Contribution paths for code, docs, research, education, and coordination
- Glossary terms needed for governance and participation conversations
