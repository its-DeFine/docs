### Body

Livepeer is a full-stack decentralised infrastructure platform and marketplace for video streaming & AI. The video streaming software is underpinned by a network of actors that perform the work needed to compute, transcode & orchestrate video & AI jobs in the Livepeer network.

Open-participation, distributed infrastructure systems like Livepeer aren't just software systems - they're multi-layered digital ecosystems that require careful design of economic incentives, governance structures, and technical architecture.

Tip: Livepeer was one of the earliest DePIN (Decentralized Public Infrastructure Network) projects in the blockchain space.

**From a marketplace perspective, Livepeer has:**
- A demand-side agent (app, platform, or operator) which brings video or AI jobs
- A supply-side agent (Orchestrators and their GPU capacity, optionally backed by separate worker processes) which performs compute and other work
- A protocol which provides the governance, payment primitives and reward system to coordinate open participation

**From a systems engineering perspective, Livepeer has:**
- A hardware layer of GPU nodes (orchestrators) that perform the compute work
- A network layer of `go-livepeer` nodes that handle job routing, verification and payment
- A software layer of services built on top of the network layer (e.g. Studio, Daydream, ComfyStream)
- A protocol layer of smart contracts that enforce the rules of the network
- An economics layer of tokenomics and game theory that aligns incentives

**Livepeer Protocol**

The Livepeer Protocol is the underlying on-chain logic that enforces the mechanisms, incentives and rules to ensure the security, reliability, cooperation and coordination of these decentralised actors for the desired network outcomes.

Protocol Services: staking, delegation, inflation & rewards, orchestrator selection, slashing, probabilistic payments, verification rules.

**Livepeer Network**

The network is the actual running system and execution layer: Orchestrators (GPU nodes), Transcoders / Workers, Gateways (aka Broadcasters), Verification processes, Job routing, Real-time AI & video compute.

Network Services: Job routing, Verification, Payment, AI inference, Video Transcoding, Real-time AI Video Streaming.

**Protocol vs Network**

| Layer | Description |
|---|---|
| Livepeer Protocol | On-chain crypto-economic incentives & coordination; staking; payments. |
| Livepeer Network | Off-chain nodes performing real-time work (transcoding, inference, routing). |
| Relationship | The network runs the compute; the protocol governs, secures, and pays it. |

Tip: The protocol determines the rules of the game while the network is the players & participants.

**On-chain vs Off-chain:**
- Livepeer protocol = Arbitrum One (L2)
- Livepeer token (LPT) = Ethereum mainnet (L1)
- Livepeer network (GPU nodes + gateways) = off-chain execution layer

**Livepeer Nodes, Actors & Roles**

A Livepeer actor is any role or entity that participates in the Livepeer protocol or network and performs actions defined by the system.

Actor cards:
- Orchestrators: GPU nodes that perform transcoding / AI jobs, earn fees + staking rewards. Roles: Perform Compute Operations, participate in governance.
- Gateways: Clients submitting jobs (video or AI) into the network. Roles: Submit jobs, route work, manage payment flows, connect to orchestrators.
- Delegators: LPT holders who stake to an orchestrator. Roles: Stake LPT, earn rewards, participate in governance and voting.
- Platforms/Workers: Services built on top of the Livepeer network. Roles: Build on top of the network, provide developer-friendly and end-user services.

Additional execution roles: Transcoders (perform video transcoding), AI Workers (execute AI inference tasks).

### Body

**Livepeer Mental Model**

Livepeer is a crypto-economic coordination protocol that secures a global, on-demand GPU network optimized for low-latency video and AI, and exposed through developer-friendly platforms and applications.

You can think of Livepeer as a decentralised serverless GPU fabric with a cryptoeconomic control plane, where services are exposed through a set of developer-friendly products and applications.

Crypto-primitive advantages include: dynamic scaling & resilience, global payment rails (accessible by AI agents), permissionless global participation & access, fairer creator economics, lower latency and data ownership, lower costs without lock-in, incentives provide runway to self-sustaining infrastructure, network effects.

**Infra Layers:**
- Protocol: A crypto-economic protocol that secures and coordinates the network (incentives, staking, governance, payments). Badge: Livepeer Protocol.
- Network: A global GPU network that performs the actual compute work (supply, routing, performance). Badges: Orchestrators, Gateways.
- Applications and Platforms: Developer-facing tools and applications (APIs, SDKs, apps). Badges: Gateways, Platforms.

**Livepeer as an OSI-Like Stack**

Uses the OSI model as a reference framework. Livepeer can be pictured as an OSI stack where the "cloud control plane" is replaced by a cryptoeconomic protocol, and persistent storage is optional, external, and secondary to real-time streamed compute.

**Network & Protocol Stack layers:**

Layer 1 — Physical Compute & Resource Layer (OSI: Physical, Badge: Orchestrators)
- GPUs, CPUs, memory, bandwidth; heterogeneous hardware; Livepeer does not own infrastructure, it indexes and incentivizes it. Provided by Orchestrators (GPU operators).

Layer 2 — Transport & Media Substrate (OSI: Data Link / Transport, Badge: Gateways)
- Video transport, chunking, codecs, streaming primitives; segment-based processing; gateway-mediated delivery. Key idea: Livepeer is built around media-native transport patterns.

Layer 3 — Distributed Execution Network (OSI: Network, Badge: Orchestrators)
- Distributed job routing + load balancing; service discovery; orchestrator selection. Key idea: The network decides who executes; the protocol incentivizes honest participation.

Layer 4 — Crypto-Economic Coordination Layer (OSI: Session / Control Plane conceptual, Badge: Protocol)
- On-chain logic that secures and coordinates the network; cryptoeconomic primitives; permissionless participation; stake-weighted influence. Key idea: This is the "brain" that replaces a centralized cloud control plane.

Layer 5 — Economic & Security Layer (OSI: No direct equivalent)
- LPT staking + delegation; inflation-based rewards; fee capture and distribution; slashing; governance processes. Key idea: Security achieved via economic incentives + cryptographic verification.

Layer 6 — Data & State Layer (OSI: Presentation / Data conceptual)
- Data plane around jobs: segments, manifests, logs, receipts; Livepeer is intentionally light on storage; long-term storage is typically external. Key idea: Livepeer coordinates compute over data, not long-term data custody.

**Platform and Application Stack layers:**

Layer 7 — Platform Services (OSI: Application Services conceptual)
- Managed services abstracting protocol/network complexity; hosted/managed gateway experiences; examples: Livepeer Studio, Streamplace, Daydream. Key idea: This is where the network meets user needs. Products are opinions about access.

Layer 8 — Developer Interfaces (OSI: Presentation developer-facing)
- APIs, SDKs, client libraries, docs; Gateway APIs; SDKs (JS, Python, etc.); BYOC-style integrations. Key idea: This layer collapses network/protocol complexity into stable developer primitives.

Layer 9 — Developer Applications (OSI: Application)
- End-user applications and services built on top of Livepeer; examples: Decentralized Twitch alternatives, AI video apps, surveillance/vision pipelines, Metaverse/XR video. Key idea: Applications are plural and replaceable; the network remains the substrate.

Layer 10 — End User (OSI: Application)
- Viewers, creators, AI consumers; end users never see orchestrators, staking/delegation, or protocol mechanics. Key idea: Success means protocol mechanics disappear behind great UX.

**Middleware & Integrations**

Livepeer replaces persistent storage coupling with streamed, ephemeral compute coordination. The "joining layer" is protocol-level orchestration, segment-based streaming, economic guarantees. Not Kafka. Not Airflow. Not Kubernetes. But conceptually: A decentralized serverless GPU fabric with a cryptoeconomic control plane.

### Body

**Overview and separation**

Livepeer is designed as a modular video/AI network anchored by a minimal on-chain protocol. The protocol manages assets and incentives on Ethereum, while the off-chain network performs the heavy lifting of transcoding and AI processing.

- On-chain protocol: smart contracts hold ETH deposits; mint and distribute LPT staking tokens; issue probabilistic payment tickets; enforce staking, slashing and reward rules; manage governance. Does not transcode video or run AI.
- Off-chain network: orchestrator nodes run Livepeer software to perform transcoding and AI workloads. Compete on price, quality and latency. Job routing happens entirely off-chain.
- Bridge: broadcasters deposit ETH and send probabilistic tickets with each segment. Orchestrators redeem winning tickets on-chain for ETH fees; work is delivered off-chain. LPT is not used to pay for services; it is only used for staking, delegation and governance.

**Definitions**

Livepeer Protocol: The ruleset + on-chain logic governing staking, delegation, inflation & rewards, orchestrator selection, slashing, probabilistic payments, verification rules. The economic and coordination layer that enforces correct behavior.

Livepeer Network: The actual running system of machines performing work: Orchestrators (GPU nodes), Transcoders / Workers, Gateways, Broadcasters, Verification processes, Job routing, Real-time AI & video compute. The live, operational decentralized GPU mesh running video + AI jobs.

Livepeer Actors: Any role or entity that participates in the Livepeer protocol or network and performs actions defined by the system. Livepeer's ecosystem involves both protocol actors (on-chain roles) and community actors (network participants and builders).

**Protocol vs Network table:**

| Layer | Description |
|---|---|
| Livepeer Protocol | On-chain crypto-economic incentives & coordination; staking; payments. |
| Livepeer Network | Off-chain nodes performing real-time work (transcoding, inference, routing). |
| Relationship | The network runs the compute; the protocol governs, secures, and pays it. |

On-chain vs Off-chain: Protocol = Arbitrum One (L2), LPT token = Ethereum mainnet (L1), Network (GPU nodes + gateways) = off-chain execution layer.

### Body

**Actor Overview**

Livepeer involves multiple actors, both on-chain (protocol) and off-chain (community).

Key roles:
- **Orchestrator**: A bonded node operator that claims work on-chain. Maintains active LPT stake, sets fee-sharing percentages, calls on-chain reward function each round. Earns ETH fees from broadcasters and LPT from inflation.
- **Transcoder / Worker**: A compute node (often a GPU) that performs the actual encoding or inference. Workers do not hold stake themselves; they operate under an Orchestrator.
- **Gateway (Broadcaster)**: A stateless demand-side node. Submits video or AI jobs to the network and pays the node that completes the work. Bridges an input stream into Livepeer, handling micropayment tickets on-chain.
- **Delegator**: A token holder who stakes LPT behind one or more orchestrators. Delegators strengthen network security and decentralization. Receive a portion of node's rewards (both mint emissions and usage fees) according to their stake.
- **Livepeer Foundation**: A non-profit entity formed to guide ecosystem growth. Coordinates long-term strategy and funding, runs community programs (like advisory boards), helps align core protocol development with open-source community.
- **Developers & Builders**: Software teams building on Livepeer. Decentralized video apps and infrastructure tools.
- **AI Artists & Content Creators**: Performers and creators who use Livepeer's tools (like Daydream) to produce content.
- **Gateway Operators**: Organizations running public Livepeer gateway services (e.g. Livepeer Cloud, Livepeer Studio).
- **Special Purpose Entities (SPEs)**: Community-approved teams funded by the on-chain treasury. Tackle public goods for Livepeer (e.g. LiveInfra, LISAR).

### Body

Actors are the participants that make the Livepeer network function economically and technically. Livepeer's ecosystem involves both protocol actors (on-chain roles) and network actors (network participants and builders).

**Core Actors**

Three main actor roles: Orchestrators, Delegators, and Gateways (formerly called Broadcasters).

- **Orchestrators (Node Operators)**: Run the go-livepeer client software, provide compute (GPU-accelerated) to transcode video streams or run AI inference jobs. Stake LPT (self-bond) to become active: higher stake = more work assigned. Set pricing parameters (ETH per pixel, fee share %, reward share %). Must call on-chain functions (like `reward`) to claim ETH fees and LPT inflation each round.
- **Delegators (Token Holders)**: LPT holders stake by delegating to one or more Orchestrators. Strengthens network security. Delegation does not transfer token ownership — LPT remains in delegator's wallet, bonded by staking contract. Receive fraction of each Orchestrator's fees and inflation. Must stay staked (and migrated to Arbitrum) to continue earning. Gain governance rights — can detach stake to vote on proposals, or vote via their Orchestrator.
- **Gateways (Broadcasters/Clients)**: Publish video streams or AI inference requests to the network. Send raw media segments to the network along with ETH to pay for services. Negotiate with Orchestrators (off-chain) to select suitable worker. Attach tickets as payment promises. Do not need LPT. Do not earn stake rewards. Are the network's demand side.

Key Role Flow: Delegators stake LPT to support Orchestrators, which powers security and gives Delegators a cut of rewards. Orchestrators register on-chain, declare capacity and pricing, then accept work from Gateways. Gateways submit video/AI jobs (paying fees) to Orchestrators for processing.

**Role Summary**

- Developers And Applications: Bring demand into the network, integrate through gateway-facing APIs, optimise for latency/quality/cost.
- Gateway Operators: Provide job intake and coordination, match workloads to available orchestrators, handle routing/retries/service-level interfaces.
- Orchestrator Operators: Provide supply-side compute participation, execute or coordinate transcoding and AI workloads, earn fees and rewards based on activity and stake backing.
- Delegators: Bond LPT to orchestrators, influence security and economic weight, share in reward and fee outcomes.

**Interaction Pattern**

Common pattern: Application -> Gateway -> Orchestrator -> Gateway -> Application

This separation improves operator specialization and system composability.

**Why Role Separation Matters**
- Better developer experience on the demand side
- Operational specialization on the supply side
- Open competition across service and compute providers
- Clearer accountability for pricing, performance, and reliability

### Body

Livepeer exposes multiple access interfaces for developers, creators, and infrastructure operators to interact with the protocol and network. Includes SDKs, REST and gRPC APIs, CLI, GraphQL endpoints, and playback tooling.

**Interface categories table:**

| Interface | Use case | Users | Access |
|---|---|---|---|
| REST API | Start sessions, control workflows | App developers, Gateways | HTTPS |
| gRPC API | Fast low-latency session control | Gateway nodes | gRPC |
| GraphQL API | Explore network, jobs, rewards | Analysts, explorers | GraphQL |
| JS SDK | Playback, ingest, session control | Frontend developers | JavaScript |
| CLI | Orchestrator & delegator control | Node operators | Terminal |
| Smart contracts | Protocol-level operations (stake, redeem, govern) | Power users, devs | Solidity / RPC |

**REST API (Livepeer Studio):** `https://livepeer.studio/api`. Endpoints: `POST /stream`, `POST /transcode`, `POST /ai/infer`, `GET /session/:id`.

**gRPC API (Gateway nodes):** High-throughput, low-latency Orchestrator routing. Methods: `ReserveSession`, `Heartbeat`, `ReportJobComplete`, `OrchestratorList`. Used by: Studio Gateway, Daydream Gateway, Cascade. Proto: gateway.proto.

**GraphQL Explorer API:** `https://explorer.livepeer.org/graphql`. Supports: delegator rewards, inflation rate, total active stake, round info.

**JS SDK:** `@livepeer/sdk`. Install via `npm install @livepeer/sdk`. Features: Ingest, AI job submit, view session output, wallet support, playback and stats.

**CLI:** Via `go install github.com/livepeer/go-livepeer`. Commands: `stake`, `unbond`, `withdraw`, `reward`, `claim`, `transcode`, `broadcast`, `query`.

**Smart contract interfaces:** BondingManager (stake, reward, unbond), TicketBroker (redeem tickets, deposit, withdraw), Governor (vote, queue, execute LIPs).

Workflow examples: transcode from web app, run AI image-to-image via curl, check node metrics with `livepeer_cli status`.

### Body

**Lifecycle Narrative (7 steps):**
1. Ingest and segmentation: Gateway receives RTMP stream and produces segments.
2. Discovery and selection: Gateway selects an Orchestrator set according to discovery logic.
3. Price and session parameters: Orchestrators advertise price per pixel (Wei denominated) off-chain.
4. Segment dispatch and compute: Gateway uploads segments; Orchestrator executes transcoding/AI compute locally or delegates to attached transcoder processes.
5. Result return and verification: Results returned to Gateway; verification may be performed; failures can trigger orchestrator swaps and retries.
6. Continuous settlement: Gateway sends probabilistic payment tickets; Orchestrator redeems winning tickets.
7. Periodic reward accounting: Each round, orchestrators may call `reward()` distributing minted rewards to itself and its delegators.

**State machine:** Idle → Ingesting → Discovering → SessionEstablished → SegmentDispatch → Executing → Returning → Verifying → Publishing → Settling → (loop or Drained/Failed). Includes RoundReward periodic branch.

**Events and transitions table** (with observable metrics):
- Stream starts → livepeer_stream_started_total increments → Idle → Ingesting
- Discovery fails → livepeer_discovery_errors_total → Discovering → Failed
- Segment transcode fails → livepeer_segment_transcode_failed_total → Executing → Retry (maxAttempts default 3)
- Orchestrator swap mid-stream → livepeer_orchestrator_swaps → Retry/Verifying → SwapOrchestrator
- Payment sent → livepeer_tickets_sent → Publishing → Settling
- Reserve/deposit depleted → livepeer_gateway_reserve low → Settling → Drained
- Ticket redemption error → livepeer_ticket_redemption_errors → Settling → (degraded)
- On-chain tx confirmation timeout → txTimeout default 5 mins → Settling/RoundReward → (retry/replace tx)
- Per-round reward minted/distributed → orchestrator reward service enabled → Publishing ↔ RoundReward

**Job Lifecycle: Video vs AI**

Transcoding Workflow: Gateway registers funds in TicketBroker → selects Orchestrator off-chain → submits segments with probabilistic payment tickets → Orchestrator passes to Transcoder (GPU) → Transcoder returns encoded segments → Orchestrator sends back to Gateway → Orchestrator redeems winning tickets on-chain.

Probabilistic Payments: Lottery-ticket scheme. Each ticket has a chance to "win" a fixed ETH prize. Over many segments, expected payout equals the true cost of work. Shields Orchestrators from tiny on-chain transactions.

AI Inference Workflow: Same stake/fee model but may involve pipelines of multiple models. Livepeer's Cascade framework coordinates multi-step AI workflows. Example: Daydream captures webcam video, sends through StableDiffusion pipeline, returns stylized video output.

Common pattern: Gateway → Orchestrator(s) → Transcoder/AI Model → Gateway.

**Mechanism Objectives:** Incentivise honest behavior, Scale supply capacity by rewarding infrastructure providers proportionally, Secure verification of off-chain video and AI workloads through bonded stake, Disincentivise dishonest actors via penalties (slashing).

**Protocol Features (Blockchain Attributes):**
- All protocol state (bonds, delegations, votes, job payments, etc.) lives on Arbitrum. LPT token is ERC-20-based and bridged between L1 and L2.
- After Confluence (LIP-73), Ethereum mainnet mainly serves as a historical source — new stake/votes are on Arbitrum only.

**Consensus & Finality:** Livepeer does not use its own consensus; relies on Arbitrum's optimistic rollup. Blocks (called rounds) occur roughly every 24 hrs, and after a ~7-day challenge window the state is finalized on Ethereum.

**Security Model:** Core contracts on Arbitrum One, inheriting Ethereum-level security via optimistic rollup. LPT stakers provide economic collateral against misbehavior. Protocol rules penalize malfeasance by slashing stake.

**Node-Level Trust:** Gateways/Broadcasters and orchestrators exchange signed data (video segments, "tickets," job outcomes). Off-chain interactions can be audited via perceptual hashes or full verification.

**AI vs Video Mechanisms:** AI services do not participate in the same reward distribution or round-based active set election as video Orchestrators. The video transcoding system and AI system are architecturally separate and operate independently.

**Incentive Structure Comparison:**

| Component | Video Orchestrators | AI Services |
|---|---|---|
| Protocol Rewards | Inflationary LPT rewards | None |
| Staking Required | Yes — LPT bonding to transcoderPool | No |
| Active Set Benefits | Stake-weighted election to active set | No active set concept |
| Fee Earnings | Yes via TicketBroker integration | None in protocol |

**Mechanism Details (Tabs):**

Rounds and Timing: Fixed length in blocks (configured in RoundsManager). New rounds must be initialized by calling `initializeRound()`. Round length: 5760 blocks for mainnet (~24 hours). Operations like staking/unbonding take effect next round. Rewards can only be claimed once per round by a transcoder.

Delegation & Staking: Orchestrators bond LPT to participate. Bonded stake weight determines proportion of new LPT minted each round an orchestrator receives. Delegators are LPT holders who delegate to an orchestrator and share in its rewards (% set by Orchestrator). Staking gives both orchestrators and delegators voting power. Misbehaviour can trigger slashing. Functions: `bond()`, `transcoder()`, `unbond()`, `withdrawStake()`.

Reward System: Service fees (paid in ETH) and inflationary LPT are pooled each round. Active Orchestrators receive rewards proportional to stake and work done. Delegators share in rewards (% set by Orchestrator). Dynamic inflation model: new LPT minted every round. If less than 50% staked, inflation increases; if more than 50% staked, inflation decreases. Rewards accumulate in earnings pools claimed by delegators through `claimEarnings()`.

Micropayment System (Probabilistic): Broadcasters fund account with deposit and reserve via `fundDepositAndReserve()`. For each video segment, broadcaster creates signed ticket with faceValue and winProb. Tickets submitted off-chain to the orchestrator. Orchestrators redeem winning tickets on-chain via `redeemWinningTicket()`. This system allows efficient microtransactions without requiring every payment to be recorded on-chain.

Upgradeability: Protocol uses standard proxy pattern. Controller maintains a registry of contract IDs to implementation addresses. Upgrades performed by deploying new implementation contract. Governor is used to update implementation address in the Controller.

Deployment and Network Configuration: Protocol deployed on Ethereum mainnet though actively used on Arbitrum One. Main configuration parameters: Number of active transcoders, Unbonding period length, Round length and lock parameters, Inflation rate and target bonding rate, Treasury reward cut rate.

### Body

Livepeer uses both LPT and ETH to power its network and compensate actors. Uses a dynamic inflation rate and stake-for-access model.

**Money Flow: LPT Token Economics**

1. Staking Flow (Delegators → Orchestrators):
   - Token Approval: delegators must approve BondingManager to transfer LPT
   - Bonding: Delegators call `Bond(amount, orchestratorAddress)` which transfers LPT to BondingManager, updates orchestrator's delegated stake, updates transcoder pool sorted by stake
   - Orchestrator Registration: node must first bond LPT to itself, call `Transcoder(rewardCut, feeShare)`, register service URI via `SetServiceURI()`

2. Reward Flow (Minter → Orchestrators → Delegators): Each round, active orchestrators call `Reward()`. Reward Calculation: BondingManager queries `CurrentMintableTokens()` from Minter; Minter calculates `mintable = inflation * totalSupply / bonded`; reward distributed proportionally; orchestrator keeps commission (rewardCut); remaining rewards distributed to delegators proportionally. Delegators must call `ClaimEarnings(endRound)` to realize accumulated rewards.

3. Payment Flow (Broadcasters → Orchestrators): Probabilistic micropayments. Broadcaster Deposits via `FundDepositAndReserve()` (Deposit + Reserve). For each video segment: broadcaster creates signed ticket with faceValue and winProb; ticket sent off-chain via HTTP headers; most tickets don't win (e.g. 1% win probability). Orchestrator validates ticket, checks if winning using `recipientRand`, queues winning tickets. On-Chain Redemption: orchestrator calls `RedeemWinningTicket(ticket, sig, recipientRand)`; TicketBroker validates and transfers `faceValue` from broadcaster's reserve to orchestrator. Achieves ~99% cost reduction vs. per-segment on-chain payments.

4. Fee Flow (Broadcasters → Orchestrators → Delegators): Fees accumulate in orchestrator's earnings pool. Orchestrator keeps commission (feeShare). Remaining fees distributed to delegators. Delegators claim via `ClaimEarnings()`.

5. Withdrawal Flows: Unstaking (Delegators): `Unbond(amount)` → wait `UnbondingPeriod` rounds (typically 7) → `WithdrawStake(unbondingLockId)`. Payment Withdrawal (Broadcasters): `Unlock()` → wait unlock period → `Withdraw()`.

**Client Integration:** go-livepeer client abstracts all contract interactions through `LivepeerEthClient` interface. Contract addresses dynamically discovered through Controller registry using Keccak256 hashes.

**Gas Optimisation Strategies:** Pool Hints (O(1) insertions), Batch Operations (only winning tickets require on-chain transactions), Dynamic Gas Pricing (2x base fee with user-specified ceilings).

**Summary: Complete Money Flow:**
1. Token holders approve and bond LPT → BondingManager (staking)
2. Minter mints new LPT → BondingManager → Orchestrators (inflation rewards)
3. Orchestrators distribute rewards → Delegators (minus commission)
4. Broadcasters deposit ETH → TicketBroker (payment reserves)
5. Orchestrators redeem tickets → receive ETH from TicketBroker (fees)
6. Orchestrators distribute fees → Delegators (minus commission)
7. Delegators claim earnings from BondingManager (accumulated rewards + fees)

Circular economy: Staking secures the network and earns inflation rewards. Active orchestrators earn fees from broadcasters. Delegators participate in rewards without running infrastructure. Probabilistic payments enable cost-efficient micropayments.

**LP Token**

Livepeer is a utility token and core component of the Livepeer Protocol. Used to secure and incentivise the decentralised network.

ELI5: LPT is akin to a membership key for Livepeer. You need it to join and earn. If you hold LPT, you can rent out your GPU (participate) or vote on network rules. Over time, the network prints new LPT to reward people who help run it. Those who have staked get extra tokens.

One of Livepeer's biggest competitive advantages is its decentralisation — creating free markets and competitive pricing. This network is underpinned by the Livepeer Token (LPT).

**Token Purpose:**
- Staking: LPT must be staked (bonded) via BondingManager contract to operate as Orchestrator or to delegate
- Governance: Any staked LPT can vote on proposals. Delegators' votes cast via their chosen Orchestrator
- Security: Protocol secured by stake. If Orchestrator misbehaves, its staked LPT (and its Delegators') can be slashed

Important: LPT is NOT used for service payments for video and AI compute — those are paid in ETH or other currencies via separate payment channels.

**LPT Usage table:**

| Use Case | LPT Functionality |
|---|---|
| Protocol Security | Bonded stake determines active Orchestrators |
| Inflation Rewards | Only bonded LPT receives newly minted token share |
| Governance | Voting rights restricted to bonded LPT holders |
| Slashing Guarantee | Orchestrators risk LPT loss for malicious behavior |
| Delegation Incentives | Delegators earn yield by bonding LPT to performant Orchestrators |

**Supply & Distribution:**
- Initial Supply: 10,000,000 LPT at genesis (2018), distributed via Merkle Mine (no ICO or pre-mine)
- Initial distribution: Community MerkleMine 63.44%, Team & Founders 19.00%, Early Backers 12.35%, Protocol Treasury 5.21%
- Current Supply: ~37,900,000 LPT (as of early 2025)
- Inflation Model: Dynamically mints new LPT. If % staked falls below 50%, inflation increases. If staking above 50%, inflation decreases.
- Example (early 2025): ~44% LPT staked, inflation rate ~25.6% APR → stakers saw ~58% effective APR
- Staked vs Unstaked: Roughly 44% staked (June 2025). Only staked portion earns inflation rewards.

**Dynamic Inflation Model:**

Inflation rule: If B(t)/S(t) < β*: r(t+1) = min(r(t) + Δ, r_max). Else: r(t+1) = max(r(t) - Δ, r_min).

Where: S(t) = total circulating supply, B(t) = total bonded supply, β* = target bonding rate (e.g. 50%), r(t) = current inflation rate, Δ = step rate (e.g. 0.05%), r_min, r_max = protocol-set bounds (e.g. 0.5% to 7%). Minting Function: M(t) = r(t) * S(t).

**Rewards Distribution:**
- Orchestrators: pro-rata by bonded stake
- Delegators: share of orchestrator rewards, split by rewardCut
- Treasury: fixed % (currently 10%) of M(t) per round

**Technical Mechanics:**

Bonding & Unbonding: Bond LPT to an orchestrator → Unbond (7-day period) → Withdraw. Rebonding (move bonded stake to another orchestrator instantly).

Slashing & Penalties: Bonded LPT subject to slashing if orchestrator submits invalid transcoding results, cheats on ticket redemption, or fails on-chain verification. Slashed LPT: partially burned, partially redirected to treasury, results in delegator collateral loss.

Livepeer uses a hybrid on-chain/off-chain governance model that combines open community discussion with binding on-chain votes. Community (token holders) collectively controls upgrades and spending, with the protocol enforcing the outcome.

**Governance Functions:** Protocol Upgrades and Treasury Spending.

**Governance Process (8 steps):**
1. Idea Phase: Community Discussion on Livepeer Forum
2. Draft Phase: Request For Feedback (RFP) — community members post pre-proposals
3. Proposal: Livepeer Improvement Proposal (LIP) — formal design document on GitHub
4. Proposal Submission: On-Chain — anyone with ≥100 LPT can submit to Governor contract (stake is returned if proposal passes)
5. Proposal Voting: On-Chain — voting period of 30 rounds (≈3.75 days) opens; eligible voters cast votes; visible on Livepeer Explorer
6. Proposal Voting Details — Voting power is LPT stake-weighted. Delegators can withdraw delegation temporarily to vote separately.
7. Proposal Voting: Quorum & Approval — at least 33% of total staked LPT participates (quorum) and >50% of votes cast are "For"
8. Proposal Execution: On-Chain — Governor automatically executes proposal's instructions

**Livepeer Improvement Proposals (LIPs):** Formal design documents hosted on GitHub, similar to Ethereum's EIPs.

- Treasury Creation: LIP-89 (established Treasury) and LIP-92 (10% of new LPT emissions into treasury)

### Body

Livepeer's original whitepaper (published 2017) outlined an ambitious design for a fully decentralized live video streaming network. Goal: build "the world's open video infrastructure", where anyone could contribute or access video encoding and delivery on-demand.

Whitepaper GitHub: https://github.com/livepeer/wiki/blob/master/WHITEPAPER.md

Livepeer was one of the earliest DePIN (Decentralized Public Infrastructure Network) projects in the blockchain space.

Key ideas:
- **Incentivized Participation** with native token (LPT) and trustless verification mechanism. Anyone running a node could perform video transcoding and be rewarded in LPT.
- **Trustless Verification:** Proposed using Truebit (a decentralized computation oracle) to verify transcoded video segments. Transcoding nodes would commit results on-chain, and a Truebit-based challenge could detect incorrect outputs.
- **Open Participation:** Fully open market of transcoders (nodes providing compute) and broadcasters (users submitting video) interacting via smart contracts. "No central party or company needs to be relied upon for the continued operation of the network."
- **Economic Security via Token:** LPT introduced as coordination mechanism — nodes would stake LPT as a performance bond to earn the right to transcode video. Staked tokens act as collateral against malicious behavior.

Note: Livepeer's token distribution had no ICO. LPT was distributed via a Merkle Mine, allowing a wide set of participants to claim tokens at network launch.

**Livepeer Now:** Livepeer today both honors and extends the original whitepaper's vision. Core idea of a decentralized video network powered by a crypto token has been proven in production — Livepeer handles millions of video minutes and has hundreds of node operators on five continents. Key technical shifts: off-chain scaling (Streamflow, Arbitrum) and expansion into GPU-based AI workloads. What began as a decentralized live streaming protocol has grown into a general platform for real-time media computing.
