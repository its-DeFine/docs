## Key Findings

### Finding 1 — Section count at upper boundary of target range

The run produced 9 sections. The v4 prompt target range is 8–12. This is within range and represents a well-justified structure for the Community tab's breadth.

Unlike the operator tabs (Orchestrators, Gateways), the Community tab serves a genuinely multi-path audience — newcomers, contributors, governance participants, funded teams, and ecosystem mappers. A 9-section structure for 5 distinct personas with 7 distinct jobs is reasonable. The section count was not flagged in the Addendum as requiring justification, and none is needed.

The section structure is:

| # | Section | purpose | pageType | lifecycleStage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pick your path into Livepeer participation | orient | navigation | discover |
| 2 | Map the community structure | explain | concept | discover |
| 3 | Join the live venues and recurring touchpoints | start | instruction | setup |
| 4 | Start contributing without funding | start | guide | setup |
| 5 | Choose the right funded path | choose | guide | evaluate |
| 6 | Follow the governance and treasury flow | explain | concept | evaluate |
| 7 | Participate in an active proposal or accountability cycle | operate | guide | operate |
| 8 | Explore the ecosystem in motion | evaluate | resource | evaluate |
| 9 | Route into deeper role-specific or reference paths | orient | navigation | operate |

Two `navigation` sections (S1 and S9) is a Community-tab-specific pattern: S1 disambiguates at entry; S9 routes out at graduation. This is structurally correct. The tab owns the participation layer — both ends of the journey require routing.

---

### Finding 2 — Arriving question is unusually specific and well-calibrated

The arriving question produced:

> "I want to get involved in Livepeer, but what is the right participation path for me right now, and what exact first step should I take?"

This is the strongest arriving question in the test suite so far. It captures the dual problem of the Community audience: not only "how do I do X" but "what X am I even doing." The specificity of "what exact first step" is notable — it correctly forces the section structure to resolve disambiguation before any instructional content.

Compare to Orchestrators and Gateways, where the arriving question was oriented toward a known technical goal. Community correctly identifies that the first reader problem is participation-path selection, not execution.

---

### Finding 3 — Persona set is genuinely multi-path and correctly scored

5 personas were defined, all scoring 6–9. The primary persona (First-time ecosystem participant, score 9) correctly drives section structure. The remaining personas (active contributor, governance participant, funded-work seeker, ecosystem mapper) are genuinely distinct in arriving state, entry vector, and exit destination — they are not role variations of the same journey.

The self-identification check is correctly done: the run identifies that "community member" does not route to a single persona and requires a dedicated disambiguation section as the first content section.

Entry blockers are correctly identified and resolved:

- Readers who do not know the difference between governance, funding, strategy, and execution structures → S2 resolves before any path-specific sections
- Readers who may assume LPT is required for all participation → S2 + S4 resolve this without blocking non-LPT participants

### Finding 4 — Forum-dependent terms handled correctly under source verification rule

The TERMINOLOGY_LOCK includes five terms with explicit `[verify-against: https://forum.livepeer.org]` markers: Grant, Bounty, RFP, Treasury Talk, Dev Call. This is correct execution of the source verification rule: these terms are central to the Community audience, are real terms in use, but their live definitions, programme structures, and criteria are forum/process-dependent and cannot be treated as stable from the glossary alone.

The distinction drawn in the Addendum — "terms valid as leads but not authoritative under the source registry" — accurately reflects the tier structure in `veracity-sources.md`, where the Livepeer Forum is rated `acceptable (primary for proposals not yet formalised as LIPs)`, not `primary` for general definitional authority.

The four suggested new sources added in the Addendum (Foundation blog post, two Advisory Boards forum posts, Workstreams forum post) are all appropriate: these governance evolution documents are not in the current source registry but are the closest available primary sources for Advisory Boards, Workstreams, and Foundation role definitions. The tier suggestion (`acceptable`) is correct.

7 jobs were defined, within the 5–7 target range (one above maximum — the run did not flag this). The jobs cover:

The slight overshoot to 7 (prompt target: 5–7) is not a prompt violation — 7 is within range. No flag needed.

## Issues Found

### MEDIUM — Persona path validation missing structural block analysis depth

The persona path validation table shows "None" in the Structural block column for all 5 personas. While this is likely accurate (the section structure is well-designed), the validation commentary is shallow for two paths:

- The **Governance participant** persona (enters at S6) passes through the structure without visiting S1–S5. The validation notes that S7 "must distinguish what is possible at pre-proposal, proposal, voting, and accountability stages" — but does not address whether S6 alone is sufficient as an entry point for a reader with no prior context of the tab. A late-entry governance participant who arrives directly at S6 skips S2 (community structure map). The knowledge gap note correctly flags this, but it should be elevated to a structural observation: S6 assumes the reader knows the community structure. Whether this is resolvable within S6 or requires a back-reference note is a design detail, not a blocker, but it warrants flagging.

- The **Ecosystem mapper** persona (enters at S2, exits at S8) has a gap: S8 ("Explore the ecosystem in motion") is marked `pageType: resource`. A resource page is a curated collection — it cannot independently resolve the ecosystem mapper's question without knowing what projects and teams are currently active. The validation notes that "S8 must provide enough current ecosystem framing to compare teams and initiatives without forcing role-tab hopping first" — but does not address the staleness risk of a resource page in this position. This is a content authoring consideration, not a structural error.

Neither issue requires a prompt change. Both are correct behaviour — they represent the kind of content authoring flags that should appear in a collation output. Note for Phase 2 planning.
