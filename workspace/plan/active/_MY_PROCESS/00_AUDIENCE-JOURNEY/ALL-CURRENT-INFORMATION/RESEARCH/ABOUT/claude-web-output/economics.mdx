Livepeer uses both its native token (LPT) and Ethereum (ETH) to power its network and compensate actors for their contributions. ETH pays for compute work performed. LPT coordinates staking, delegation, governance, and inflation-based participation incentives.

The two income streams are distinct in origin and in what they measure. Fee revenue in ETH reflects actual network usage - video transcoding demand and AI inference demand. Inflation rewards in LPT reflect the protocol's current phase: bootstrapping participation and security before fee revenue is sufficient to sustain the network on its own. Both matter, and their ratio matters. Current metric values are governance-controlled and change between rounds - verify live figures at [explorer.livepeer.org](https://explorer.livepeer.org).

<CustomDivider middleText="Staking Flow" />

## 1. Staking flow (Delegators to Orchestrators)

Staking bonds LPT from token holders into the BondingManager contract, where it backs an orchestrator's position in the active set and earns inflation rewards.

**Token approval**: Before bonding, delegators approve the BondingManager to transfer their LPT tokens via the standard ERC-20 approval pattern.

**Bonding**: Delegators call `Bond(amount, orchestratorAddress)`, which transfers LPT from the delegator to the BondingManager, updates the orchestrator's total delegated stake, and adjusts the orchestrator's position in the transcoder pool. The pool is sorted by delegated stake and maintained as a linked list; callers supply position hints to keep insertions at O(1) cost.

**Orchestrator registration**: To become an active orchestrator, a node must first bond LPT to itself, then call `Transcoder(rewardCut, feeShare)` to register commission parameters, and set a service URI via `SetServiceURI()`. The service URI is how gateways discover and connect to the orchestrator off-chain.

{/* REVIEW: The transcoder pool size (active set cap) is governance-controlled. Verify current active set size at explorer.livepeer.org */}

<CustomDivider middleText="Reward Flow" />

## 2. Reward flow (Minter to Orchestrators to Delegators)

Each round, active orchestrators call `Reward()` on the BondingManager to mint their share of the round's inflationary token issuance.

**Reward calculation**:

1. Orchestrator calls `Reward()` on BondingManager.
2. BondingManager queries `CurrentMintableTokens()` from the Minter.
3. Minter calculates: `mintable = currentInflation * totalSupply / totalBonded` - proportional to the orchestrator's share of total delegated stake.
4. Reward is distributed to the orchestrator's earnings pool.
5. Orchestrator retains their `rewardCut` commission.
6. Remaining rewards accumulate proportionally for delegators.

**Claiming earnings**: Delegators call `ClaimEarnings(endRound)` to realise accumulated rewards and fees from past rounds.

**Inflation mechanics**: The `currentInflation` rate adjusts each round toward `targetBondingRate`. When the fraction of LPT bonded falls below the target, `currentInflation` rises by `inflationChange` per round. When it exceeds the target, the rate falls.

{/* REVIEW: targetBondingRate is governance-controlled. LIP-107 (voted February 2026) proposes reducing it from 50% to 46% with an accelerated inflationChange. Verify current parameter values at explorer.livepeer.org/governance and the Livepeer Forum. */}

{/* REVIEW: LIP-100 proposes adding inflation ceiling and floor bounds. Verify whether this has passed and what bounds are in effect at explorer.livepeer.org/governance. */}

<CustomDivider middleText="Payment Flow" />

## 3. Payment flow (Gateways to Orchestrators)

The payment system uses probabilistic micropayments to settle compute fees without a per-segment on-chain transaction.

**Gateway deposits**: Gateways fund their account via `FundDepositAndReserve()` on the TicketBroker contract:
- **Deposit**: Locked collateral that cannot be used immediately; protects against front-running.
- **Reserve**: Available pool from which winning ticket redemptions are paid.

**Off-chain ticket creation**: For each video segment or AI inference job processed, the gateway creates a signed ticket with a `faceValue` and `winProbability`. The ticket is sent off-chain to the orchestrator alongside the job. Most tickets do not win - a 1% win probability means one in one hundred tickets results in a redemption.

**Ticket validation and queuing**: The orchestrator validates the ticket's signature and expiration, calculates whether the ticket wins using a `recipientRand`, and queues winning tickets in a local database for redemption. The gateway's available reserve (`MaxFloat`) is tracked to ensure sufficient funds exist before accepting additional tickets.

**On-chain redemption**: The orchestrator calls `RedeemWinningTicket(ticket, sig, recipientRand)` on the TicketBroker. The contract validates the ticket against on-chain state, transfers `faceValue` from the gateway's reserve to the orchestrator, and updates `claimedReserve` in the BondingManager. This achieves approximately 99% reduction in on-chain transaction volume compared with per-segment settlement.

<CustomDivider middleText="Fee Flow" />

## 4. Fee flow (Orchestrators to Delegators)

Fees earned from ticket redemptions accumulate in the orchestrator's earnings pool and are distributed to delegators proportionally.

- Fees accumulate in the orchestrator's earnings pool each round.
- The orchestrator retains their `feeShare` commission.
- Remaining fees are distributed to delegators in proportion to their bonded stake.
- Delegators claim accumulated fees and rewards together via `ClaimEarnings()`.

<CustomDivider middleText="Withdrawal Flows" />

## 5. Withdrawal flows

**Unstaking (delegators)**:

1. Call `Unbond(amount)` - creates an unbonding lock.
2. Wait the `unbondingPeriod` (governance-controlled; verify current value at explorer.livepeer.org).
3. Call `WithdrawStake(unbondingLockId)` - returns LPT to the delegator's wallet.

{/* REVIEW: unbondingPeriod is governance-controlled. Verify the current number of rounds at explorer.livepeer.org before documenting a specific value. */}

**Payment withdrawal (gateways)**:

1. Call `Unlock()` on TicketBroker to begin the unlock period.
2. Wait the unlock period.
3. Call `Withdraw()` to receive remaining deposit and reserve balances.

<CustomDivider middleText="Economic Reality" />

## Economic context

Livepeer's economic health is tracked through the ratio of inflation rewards to fee revenue. In Q3 2025, fee revenue was approximately $204K per quarter against approximately $18M in staking rewards - a ratio of roughly 89:1 in favour of inflation. AI inference fee revenue grew 21x over three consecutive quarters through Q3 2025, with AI workloads now accounting for over 72% of fee revenue. Fee growth is accelerating; the ratio is improving.

{/* REVIEW: These figures are from the Messari Q3 2025 Livepeer report. Verify against the most recent Messari quarterly report and explorer.livepeer.org for updated figures before publishing. */}

LIP-107, LIP-100, and LIP-101 are the first governance actions explicitly aimed at bringing inflation and fee revenue into better long-run alignment. The long-run model is a network where fee revenue from usage sustains orchestrators and delegators without dependence on continuous token dilution. The current inflation-heavy model is the bootstrapping phase, not the target state.

<CustomDivider middleText="Summary" />

## Complete money flow

The full economic cycle runs as follows:

1. Token holders approve and bond LPT through BondingManager (staking).
2. Minter mints new LPT each round and distributes it via BondingManager to orchestrators (inflation rewards).
3. Orchestrators distribute a portion of rewards to delegators after retaining rewardCut.
4. Gateways deposit ETH into TicketBroker (payment reserves).
5. Orchestrators redeem winning tickets and receive ETH from TicketBroker (usage fees).
6. Orchestrators distribute a portion of fees to delegators after retaining feeShare.
7. Delegators claim accumulated rewards and fees via `ClaimEarnings()`.

Staking secures the network and earns inflation rewards. Active orchestrators earn fees from gateways. Delegators participate in both streams without running infrastructure. Probabilistic payments enable cost-efficient micropayments at scale.
