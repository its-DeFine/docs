<Tip>
  Gateways filter orchestrators by price before routing jobs. An orchestrator priced above a gateway's cap receives no work from that gateway. An orchestrator priced below operating costs earns less than it spends. Both extremes produce zero net income.
</Tip>

Pricing operates in two domains: **video transcoding** (a single global price per pixel) and **AI inference** (per-pipeline, per-model pricing in `aiModels.json`). Reward cut and fee cut determine how inflation rewards and job fees are split between the operator and delegators.

<CustomDivider middleText="Video Pricing" />

## Video transcoding price

Video transcoding is priced in **wei per pixel**. The pixel count for a job is `width x height x output_renditions`.

Set `-pricePerUnit` at startup:

```bash icon="terminal" filename="video-price"
livepeer \
  -orchestrator \
  -transcoder \
  -pricePerUnit 1000 \
  -pixelsPerUnit 1 \
  ...
```

{/* VERIFY: Current active network price range at tools.livepeer.cloud. The 500-2,000 wei range is from March 2026 research and may have shifted. */}

`-pixelsPerUnit` is the denominator. Leave it at `1` unless expressing price over a larger pixel batch for readability.

### USD-denominated video pricing

Append the `USD` suffix to price in dollars. go-livepeer converts to wei at runtime using a Chainlink ETH/USD price feed on Arbitrum:

```bash icon="terminal" filename="usd-video-price"
-pixelsPerUnit 1000000000000 \
-pricePerUnit 0.41USD
```

This sets $0.41 per trillion pixels. Adjust `pixelsPerUnit` to keep `pricePerUnit` as a readable decimal.

### autoAdjustPrice

By default, go-livepeer adjusts the advertised price based on estimated ticket redemption overhead. When gas prices rise, the adjustment compensates:

| Overhead | Base price 1,000 wei | Advertised price |
|----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| 1% | 1,000 wei | 1,010 wei |
| 20% | 1,000 wei | 1,200 wei |
| 50% | 1,000 wei | 1,500 wei |

Disable with `-autoAdjustPrice=false` when advertising a fixed rate for commercial relationships. For general network participation, keep the default on.

### Surveying the market

Check [Livepeer Explorer](https://explorer.livepeer.org/orchestrators) for the current price distribution across the active set. Start in the lower half of currently active prices, then adjust upward after confirming that job volume holds.

### aiModels.json pricing example

```json icon="code" filename="aiModels.json"
{
  "pipeline": "text-to-image",
  "model_id": "SG161222/RealVisXL_V4.0_Lightning",
  "price_per_unit": 4768371,
  "pixels_per_unit": 1,
  "warm": true
}
```

The `price_per_unit` field accepts an integer (wei) or a USD string (e.g. `"0.5e-3USD"` with `"currency": "USD"`).

### LLM pricing

LLM pricing uses USD notation with `pixels_per_unit` as a token-count proxy:

```json icon="code" filename="llm-pricing"
{
  "pipeline": "llm",
  "model_id": "meta-llama/Meta-Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct",
  "price_per_unit": 0.18,
  "currency": "USD",
  "pixels_per_unit": 1000000,
  "url": "http://llm_runner:8000",
  "warm": true
}
```

This sets $0.18 per million tokens -- a competitive rate for 8B parameter models.

### Checking competitive AI rates

Visit [tools.livepeer.cloud/ai/network-capabilities](https://tools.livepeer.cloud/ai/network-capabilities) and filter by pipeline. Orchestrators priced above gateway caps receive no work for that pipeline. Start at or below the median, then adjust based on job volume.

## Reward cut and fee cut

These two on-chain settings control how inflation rewards and job fees are split between the operator and delegators.

<StyledTable variant="bordered">
  <thead>
    <TableRow header>
      <TableCell header>Setting</TableCell>
      <TableCell header>What it controls</TableCell>
      <TableCell header>Operator perspective</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>**Reward cut**</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Percentage of LPT inflation the orchestrator keeps before distributing the remainder to delegators</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Higher reward cut = more LPT retained per round. Lower reward cut = more attractive to delegators, increasing total stake and active set rank.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>**Fee cut**</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Percentage of ETH service fees the orchestrator keeps before distributing the remainder to delegators</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Most operators set fee cut high (90-100%) because ETH fees compensate for operational costs. Delegators prioritise reward cut over fee cut.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
  </tbody>
</StyledTable>

### Setting cuts at activation

Cuts are set during the `livepeer_cli` activation flow. Starting values:

| Setting | Recommended start | Reasoning |
|---------|------------------|-----------|
| Reward cut | 10% | Keeps 10% of minted LPT; delegates 90% to attract stake |
| Fee cut | 95% | Retains most ETH fees to cover operating costs |

### Adjusting cuts after activation

Change cuts at any time via `livepeer_cli`:

```bash icon="terminal" filename="update-cuts"
docker exec -it livepeer-orchestrator livepeer_cli
# Select: Set orchestrator config
```

{/* VERIFY: Confirm whether cut changes take effect immediately or at the next round boundary against https://github.com/livepeer/go-livepeer */}

### The operator's trade-off

Cuts balance two competing goals:

1. **Maximise personal retention** -- higher cuts mean more LPT and ETH per round.
2. **Attract delegation** -- lower reward cuts attract delegators, increasing total stake, improving active set rank, and increasing the absolute value of the operator's share (a smaller percentage of a larger reward pool).

For video transcoding, where routing favours higher-stake nodes, attracting delegation through competitive reward cuts directly increases job volume. For AI inference, where routing is capability-based, delegation matters less for job acquisition but still affects LPT inflation income.

<Note>
  Check competing orchestrators' cuts on [Livepeer Explorer](https://explorer.livepeer.org/orchestrators) before setting yours. A reward cut far above the network median deters delegators; a reward cut far below leaves earnings on the table.
</Note>

## Checking against gateway MaxPrice

Gateways configure a maximum price they will pay per capability. An orchestrator priced above a gateway's cap receives zero jobs from that gateway, regardless of hardware quality or stake position.

For video: check your `-pricePerUnit` against prices listed on [Livepeer Explorer](https://explorer.livepeer.org/orchestrators). Active orchestrators receiving work are priced within gateway acceptance ranges.

For AI: check your per-pipeline `price_per_unit` against the pricing column on [tools.livepeer.cloud/ai/network-capabilities](https://tools.livepeer.cloud/ai/network-capabilities). Pipelines with no job volume at your price tier indicate pricing above the gateway cap.
