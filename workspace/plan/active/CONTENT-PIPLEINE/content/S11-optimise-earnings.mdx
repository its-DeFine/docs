<Tip>
  Identify the single variable most limiting your earnings, adjust it, and measure the result over one round cycle before changing anything else. Changing multiple levers simultaneously makes it impossible to attribute the outcome.
</Tip>

Your node is operating and monitored. This page covers the levers available for increasing job volume, improving margins, and maximising throughput.

<CustomDivider middleText="Earnings Model" />

## Two income streams

Orchestrators earn from two independent sources:

1. **Service fees (ETH)** — paid per job by gateways through probabilistic micropayments. Determined by your pricing, your job volume, and ticket redemption efficiency.
2. **Inflation rewards (LPT)** — minted per round via the `reward()` call. Determined by your total bonded stake (self-stake plus delegated stake) and whether the reward call succeeds every round.

Optimisation targets one or both streams. The levers differ.

<CustomDivider middleText="Pricing Adjustment" />

## Pricing

Pricing is the highest-impact lever for service fee income. Gateways apply a hard MaxPrice gate — an orchestrator priced above the threshold receives zero work from that gateway.

### Video transcoding pricing

Check the current market range on [Livepeer Explorer](https://explorer.livepeer.org/orchestrators) by comparing prices across the active set. Position your `-pricePerUnit` in the lower half of active prices when starting or when job volume is low. Increase gradually as your performance score strengthens.

{/* VERIFY: Confirm current active price range on Explorer or tools.livepeer.cloud. The range shifts with ETH price and gateway cap changes. */}

To price in USD (auto-converted to wei via Chainlink price feed):

```bash icon="terminal" filename="usd-pricing"
livepeer \
  -pricePerUnit 0.41USD \
  -pixelsPerUnit 1000000000000 \
  ...
```

The `autoAdjustPrice` feature (enabled by default) adjusts your advertised price upward when gas costs for ticket redemption rise. Disable it only if you want a fixed price regardless of gas conditions:

```bash icon="terminal" filename="disable-auto-adjust"
-autoAdjustPrice=false
```

### AI inference pricing

AI pricing is set per pipeline and per model in `aiModels.json` via the `price_per_unit` field. Each pipeline has a different unit definition (pixels for image pipelines, tokens for LLM).

Check market rates on [tools.livepeer.cloud](https://tools.livepeer.cloud) and compare your per-pipeline prices against other active orchestrators serving the same models.

<CustomDivider middleText="Stake Positioning" />

## Stake and delegation

Total bonded stake (self-stake plus delegated) determines active set rank for video transcoding and influences routing probability. Higher stake attracts more video transcoding jobs through stake-weighted gateway selection.

{/* VERIFY: Confirm stake-weighted selection is still the primary video routing mechanism. [source: go-livepeer gateway selection logic] */}

### Increasing self-stake

Bond additional LPT to your own address through `livepeer_cli` or directly via the BondingManager contract. More self-stake improves your active set position immediately at the next round boundary.

### Attracting delegators

Delegators evaluate orchestrators on four criteria:

<Warning>
  Frequent or large changes to reward cut and fee cut reduce delegator confidence. If your delegator base is substantial, communicate changes through Discord or the Livepeer Forum before the on-chain transaction.
</Warning>

<Note>
  AI inference routing does not use stake-weighted selection. AI job volume is driven by capability advertisement, pricing, and warm model availability — not stake rank. Operators focused solely on AI earnings should prioritise the AI levers below over stake positioning.
</Note>

<CustomDivider middleText="AI Throughput" />

## AI model warmth and VRAM allocation

For AI operators, warm model selection is the primary throughput lever. Gateways track first-response latency — nodes with warm models serving immediate responses win more jobs than nodes with cold-start delays.

### Warm the highest-demand model

Check [tools.livepeer.cloud/ai/network-capabilities](https://tools.livepeer.cloud/ai/network-capabilities) weekly to identify which models active gateways are requesting most. Warm the model with the highest demand for your pipeline type.

To rotate the warm model, update `aiModels.json` and restart the AI runner container:

```json icon="code" filename="aiModels.json"
[
  {
    "pipeline": "text-to-image",
    "model_id": "SG161222/RealVisXL_V4.0_Lightning",
    "price_per_unit": 4768371,
    "warm": true
  },
  {
    "pipeline": "text-to-image",
    "model_id": "ByteDance/SDXL-Lightning",
    "price_per_unit": 4768371,
    "warm": false
  }
]
```

{/* VERIFY: Confirm one-warm-model-per-GPU Beta constraint is still in effect. [source: ai-runner release notes] */}

### VRAM allocation strategy

A 24 GB GPU holds one large diffusion model warm. Pair it with a VRAM-efficient pipeline (Whisper at ~3 GB, BLIP at ~1-2 GB) on a multi-GPU system to serve two revenue streams simultaneously.

### Optimisation flags for diffusion models

Apply to warm diffusion models only (`text-to-image`, `image-to-image`, `upscale` with `"warm": true`):

- **SFAST** — compiles the model compute graph on first run. Up to 25% faster inference after the initial compilation pass. No quality reduction.
- **DEEPCACHE** — caches intermediate diffusion steps. Up to 50% faster inference with minor quality reduction at high step counts. Do not use with Lightning or Turbo model variants — they are already step-optimised.

```json icon="code" filename="aiModels.json-sfast"
{
  "pipeline": "text-to-image",
  "model_id": "SG161222/RealVisXL_V4.0_Lightning",
  "price_per_unit": 4768371,
  "warm": true,
  "optimization_flags": { "SFAST": true }
}
```

<Warning>
  SFAST and DEEPCACHE cannot be combined. Set only one or neither per model entry.
</Warning>

<CustomDivider middleText="Capacity Tuning" />

## Session capacity

A `-maxSessions` value set too low wastes GPU capacity and limits earnings. Set too high, it pushes transcoding behind live segment cadence, degrades performance score, and costs you more jobs than it gains.

Use `livepeer_bench` to measure your hardware limit and set `-maxSessions` to `min(hardware_limit, bandwidth_limit)` with 20% headroom.

After tuning, monitor `livepeer_transcode_duration_seconds` in Prometheus. If this stays below 2 seconds per session, there is room to increase. If it exceeds 2 seconds consistently, reduce the limit.
